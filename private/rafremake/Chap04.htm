The Rich & the Fabulous: The Remake

by Bunny Ears

Chapter Four – Mr. Tight Ass

****************

 

Gillian was ten minutes early to law class but much to her surprise a group of students were already there, lounging in the corner. Perhaps, they were early birds just like her? The thought made her smile because that meant at least they seemed pretty serious about their education, which also meant they weren't the 'rich blokes' Charlene kept complaining about being dominant of the university.

 

Although, flattering in its own title as an elite-class law school, the place was owned by wealthy and well-known families that basically ruled the campus. Needless to say, their children usurped the priority of being a student here. How else could they have been accepted given that their intelligent factors, in Gillian's opinion, were not even passable for High School? It made her feel sorry for those who had their chances lessened because of these ungrateful snobs.

 

Thoughts swarmed away when Gillian caught a glimpse of Ruby. Even though they were roommates, their confrontation the other day was anything but pleasant. Up until this moment, Gillian could still feel the thrashing rage inside her blood. It wasn't likely that these girls could clash together easily.

 

Charlene came strolling inside the class in a style too suave that Gillian had to smile.

 

"So, you're an early bird too," Gillian remarked as the amusement lit her eyes. "No offence, but I didn't think you would be one."

 

Charlene rolled her eyes. "What's that supposed to mean, darling? I may take awhile to prepare but face it, what girl doesn't want to look her best at all times?" She flipped her hair with a hand, and Gillian realized that her fingernails were perfectly manicured. Of course. "That doesn't mean I'm one of those blokes, though."

 

Even though Charlene had used that word too many times, the humor in it was still effective and Gillian had to suppress a laugh. "You know, I wouldn't expect them to be this early either. Hell, even Ruby's here. That's the last thing I would expect."

 

Charlene's eyes turned dark with an ominous hue. "Oh, you don't know yet, Gillian. The professor is one strict man. A second late, and he'll report you to the headmaster, rich or not."

 

"And the blokes are afraid of him?" Gillian couldn't suppress the question.

 

"Oh, everyone's afraid of him," Charlene said darkly. "The folks may be rich but once the news that they fail his class reaches their parents, hell would break loose."

 

Gillian didn't quite understand what she meant by that but before she could launch another query, Ruby Lin was steering her four-inch heels their way. A cross look was stretching her face in a way it made her look disproportionate. "Oh, if it isn't my precious roommate, Kayla. How's it going?" She said, bearing a fake smile.

 

"It's Gillian," Gillian said forcibly. The fact that Ruby thought she could manipulate her was something her pride the size of Africa didn't need. If anything, she detested being treated lowly and without proper respect. "Are you sure your brain's all right? 'Cause last time I checked, didn't we just saw each other no more than fifteen minutes ago?"

 

Ruby was straining the smile but the rising anger could be detected in her darkening eyes. "Don't get cocky, bitch. You're stepping in my territory and unless you want your room trashed with hells know what, I suggest you stop your bitchiness."

 

Already, the only description Gillian could draw about the girl in front of her was that she was no different from a blonde. "Hello, trashing my room means troubles for you too, you moron. Think about it."

 

Charlene burst out laughing. "Give it up, Rubes. You're not winning this time either."

 

Ruby's mouth twitched to say something but a voice cut through hers like a bullet exploding with a fresh burst of air.

 

"Hey, Gillian!" The sight of Daniel Wu almost put a halt to Gillian's breathing. Sure, she had expected that he attended the same university like the rest of the folks here, but sharing the same class with him was entirely something else. She saw the unmistakable joy in his eyes as he sauntered his way over, followed by none other than his best friend, Edison Chen. Another surprise.

 

"What a coincidence," Gillian said.

 

"Maybe it's fate." The grin on Daniel's face was so wide it was obvious his glee was genuine. It made Gillian feel a little better about this place, given that she had been confronted by a fake bitch. "I can't believe we're actually attending the same university, and having the same class! How come I never seen you around before?"

 

She was about to explain when Ruby intervened, all her earlier feistiness disappeared in a snap. "Edison, you look fascinating! But then again, you always do," she said sweetly, clinging onto him like a magnet. "Mmm, do I smell maple syrup?"

 

"Bug off, Ruby," hissed Edison. His attention turned to Gillian, and she was sure the unpleasantness was there in his eyes. "What are you--"

 

The bell alarmed, hinting that class was starting. The professor marched into the class at the very moment, his face glum as though he was born with no other facial expression.

 

Everyone quickly rushed to their seats before the professor could give them hell. Edison seemed like he wanted to pull Gillian out and interrogate her but he would have to save it for later.

 

The professor, Joe Ma, droned into his lecture so it was dead silent except for his monotonous voice. Gillian pulled out her notebook but in the process of doing so, her wooden number two pencil came rolling out of her bag and dropped to the floor with a resounding clank. As if that wasn't enough, it continued rolling down the steps until finally it had to decide to stop at the very touch of the professor's shiny shoes.

 

He stopped his speech for a moment and gawked at the pencil at his feet, displeasure wrinkling his face. He bent down and picked up the pencil, strolling over to her desk with his heavy footsteps pounding the floor. "Is this yours?"

 

"Yes. Sorry about that, sir," said Gillian with every ounce of respect she could muster as she retrieved the pencil. She heard a snicker coming from two desks behind her and could tell it came straight from Ruby.

 

"Please uphold your belongings in the future because I do not want something as minor and as unworthy as a pencil to interrupt my gold-piece of lecture, understand?" He said it with a condemning supremacy that Gillian could not believe. He allowed her no time to rebuke on defense whatsoever before he automatically resumed his speech.

 

An hour and a half passed.

"If you have a sexual harassment case, the accused is a thirty-year old man who has a record spit clean like an unused bathroom. The victim, a twenty-five year old woman charged him, claiming he had groped her thighs while she was waiting for her train. Here are the evidences." Professor Joe Ma projected some slides onto the white board. "The only evidence is the security video, where it had exactly captured him violating her privacy. But there are no eyewitness accounts, and the place was chaotic. He denied the accusation. If you were the prosecutor lawyer, how would you handle this case?"

Gillian looked around. Nobody volunteered to answer his question.

"You, right there."

Gillian looked around once more. “Me?”

"Yes, Miss..."

"Gillian Chung."

"Miss Chung. If you were the prosecutor of this case, how would you handle the case?" He offered to repeat.

"Oh umm..." she gathered her thoughts, a bit nervous. "Well... Judging from his character, if he is a righteous, reserved man that must means he must not have the every day's advantage to flirt, touch, or have any sort of sexual contact with any woman. And from this, we could simply argue on how a man's hormones could drive him to violate a female's privacy. Surely a flash of some good thighs could make any man drool, let alone, one that barely sees any, we all know that," she grinned confidently.

"That is an inferred fact, you don't know if he had any sexual exploits. He could have sexed a woman on his kitchen counter and none of us would know." He responded, expecting her to agree.

"But that's something a lawyer has to do his⁄her job to convince the Supreme. Everything doesn't necessary have to have facts all spread out and black and white. It could be gray; it could be hidden. As a lawyer, we have to surge all the possibilities that infiltrate every aspect and present it in the most persuasive manner." Gillian argued.

"A lawyer's job is not presenting unproven theories. We all know in the science world, those hypotheses that are not supported, or proven will be rejected. Same goes for laws. You can't make a statement on behalf of psychology. You can't just bring up any random personal inferences and expect the Judge to believe it. Hell, one wrong impression from you and you lose. You can't win against anybody if you don't win over the Judge." Joe Ma fired back at her harshly.

"But psychology is a study, and if you present it at the most appropriate lights, you can win over the Judge!" Gillian rebuked, getting pissed off.

The class was watching with interest. Nobody ever dared to go against Joe Ma. He was known for biting students' heads off.

"If all you think about is winning over the Judge, what about the jury?"

"The Jury is known for being irrational, ill-formed, and emotional! We all know most verdicts made in courts are unjust yet it's not debatable! They're normal people, they have feelings; they are incapable of understanding the complexity of the legal systems. We don't have to spend time on them because after all, they're like us and obviously will tilt to the side they would feel closely related to, experienced before, etc. and emotional appeals are precisely the perfect tactic."

"That is just like saying voters are irrational, ill-formed, and emotional!" Joe Ma yelled. "You can't base things on emotional appeals. You have to use logical appeals, do you understand?" He pointed to his brain, as if to insinuate she was slow. "A court is where you bring evidences and truth to light! Not some crappy psychological views or whatever you were saying."

Gillian fumed. "But--"

"Enough." He cut her off. "Class, your homework tonight is to write how you will handle this case if you were the prosecutor. Fifteen pages. I want it outlined, rough drafted, with a bibliography, footnotes, everything. It's due next time we meet. Class dismissed."

Gillian shot up from her seat in steams, packing up her belongings.

"Wow, she dared to go against Professor Ma. She is so cool!" Daniel remarked to Edison, admiring Gillian.

Edison rolled his eyes. "Oh please, she was just being plain stupid."

**************

 

Gillian had never been gladder to be dismissed from class. Charlene excused herself to head off to the restroom so Gillian was strolling down the hallways by herself when Edison yanked her to the side with a force she had to wince.

 

"What the hell is your problem?" Gillian hissed with annoyance as she violently shook his grip off. Throughout the class, she had noticed that Edison was giving her dirty looks. She had to suppress the urge to punch his face but now he was even pissing her off further.

 

"What is MY problem?" Edison said with extra emphasis. "I think the question should be: What is YOUR problem? Why are you even here in the first place?"

 

"Why can't I be?" Gillian rebuked. The way he said it seemed to insinuate she had some bad intentions.

 

"Oh, don't put up that innocent act. I've dated enough evil bitches to know you're one of them. What is your plan?" Edison shot her a threatening look that said he was going to bestow her some real capital punishment if she dared lie or disobey. A stupid gesture that Gillian couldn't help but roll her eyes at.

 

"I don't care what type of bitches you've dated, but I don't have any plan to murder your damn friend, okay?!" Gillian pushed him away from her with no mercy spared.

 

But Edison wasn't that weak and rootless feather she had thought him to be. "You're lying! You should be in the States attending school, not here! There must be a reason why you came to Hong Kong, meeting Daniel on purpose but not telling him your his biological sister? What do you want, you tramp? Enlighten me on that one please!"

 

"Are you done barking?" Gillian hissed.

 

"No!" Edison shot back, his eyes a pit of accusations. "I can see what type of woman you are! You are the sinister, manipulative, and seductive-“

 

Gillian raised an eyebrow, amused. “So you find me seductive… Why, thank you.”

 

"No!" Edison bawled.

 

"But you just described me that way --"

 

"Stop flattering yourself, bitch!" Edison said with an intolerant hiss of indignation. "I'm not going to let you stay here. Do you know what kind of thoughts Dan is having for you? My God!"

 

Gillian's lips twitched. His outburst certainly came out as a surprise.

 

"--and he's planning to court you! What are you trying to do? Make him commit incest?" Edison's outrage continued to elevate as his eyes demanded concrete answers from her. Whoever knew that this jerk was such a loyal dolt? Of course it was commendable but Gillian refused to acknowledge such things.

 

"Look, I know what I'm doing." Gillian stated with pounds of calmness and firmness. "Even if Dan wants to commit incest, you think I will be dumb enough to let him?" Gillian rolled her eyes rudely as she was saying, "…and how many times do I have to say it's none of your damn business? Take a little advice. Next time you want to interrogate me, make sure you got a damn inspector license, bastard."

 

Suddenly, Charlene appeared from the restroom and trotted over to the two, bafflement and curiosity glowed like gold in her eyes. It was an everyday situation to see girls flirting with Edison like there was no end. Sometimes one had to wonder how in the world he enjoyed the company of women so much when all he bore in his heart for them was abhorrence and detest. But Charlene had to do a double take when she sensed a fiery tension as though they were in a dispute. "Edison, Gillie’s your new girl?" Charlene chuckled.

 

"You have got to be fucking kidding me," Edison hissed back, surprising Charlene a bit. "Hell must turn loose if I'm ever interested in this sinister, manipulative--" Edison paused when he realized he was going to make the same mistake of calling her seductive again," bitch." He finished.

 

"Whoa." Charlene looked amused and glanced over to Gillian. "You killed his dad?"

 

"Watch it!" Edison gnarled.

 

"It's just a misunderstanding," Gillian clarified.

 

"Misunderstanding?" Edison shrieked, not believing her cunningness. She made herself appeared so innocent in front of everybody! Well, Edison wasn't going to let her fool him. Her attempt to conceal this matter gave him all the more reason to believe she had something hidden up in her slinky sleeves. All women were like that.

 

"Loosen up, Mr. Tightass," Charlene said. "So you two knew each other?"

 

"Don't put me in such a position! I don't want to die ten years earlier," Edison unpleasantly said.

 

As if the situation wasn't bad enough, Daniel Wu just had to make his appearance. "Hey, what's going on here?" He asked with a smile on his face. He nodded towards Gillian in greeting which made her winced a bit when she recalled Edison's disturbing words.

 

Students who passed by couldn't help but poke their nosy noses in when they caught sight of the popular Edison and Daniel together talking with a girl they didn't know. Of course this meant some new juicy gossip to be talked about during lunch and to brush off this given invitation was a sin.

 

"I don't know." Charlene answered with a light shrug. "And I don't think I want to." Daniel's expression merged with confusion, waiting for someone to enlighten him.

 

"Huh? Am I missing something?" Daniel asked. "What's wrong?"

 

"Oh shut up," Edison gnawed at Daniel's curiosity that sliced off the last branch of his tolerance. He knew why Daniel was giving him this don't-tell-me-what-I-think-you're-doing look. Of course, the suspicious Daniel was thinking that he was trying to steal a chance with this brat. What a joke!

 

"How come the weather didn't say anything about a rainstorm?" Charlene asked, referring to Edison's exploded anger.

 

"A rainstorm?" Daniel asked in bafflement. "It's sunny like heck."

 

"Yeah," Charlene said without much feeling. Gillian had to smile at that. The girl was so subtle with her jokes.

 

"Well, Gillian, do you want to join us for lunch?" Daniel initiated. Edison's eyes amplified to the size of the earth at the sinful invitation. "Rubes got us a reservation."

 

"Hey, hey, hey," Edison interrupted, knowing he had to do something to vanquish this potential mistake-to-be. "Remember, Dan, we have a fair agreement." He said, referring to their fair competition deal. It meant that if Daniel wanted to invite Gillian to lunch, Edison had to come along.

 

Charlene's eyebrows arched as her interest was sparked. "What is this I smell?"

 

"Crap." Edison replied flatly, rolling his eyes.

 

Charlene smiled, her suspicion confirmed. "Ah, wait 'til Ruby hears this."

 

Gillian was confused at what was happening. There was certainly some sort of weirdness going on between Daniel and Edison. What "agreement" were they talking about? "Uh, someone care to elaborate?"

 

"No." Edison responded brusquely. "Mind your own business!" He mimicked her.

 

Gillian shot him a trenchant glare, but since Daniel was here, she didn't feel like blowing up. "So, let's go." She said, making Daniel burst into a huge smile.

 

With that the group sped down the hallway out of the entrance, leaving Edison all alone and resentful. The second Daniel appeared that girl was mute as a rabbit and innocent as an angel. Edison knew from the start that this girl was bad news. His veins rumbled just at the thought of her.

 

“I’ll bust you out. Just you watch,” Edison swore to himself.
