Chapter Eleven: Confessions

 

Gillian sucked in a breath and washed her face with cold water, as if it could defer reality into a nightmare. But no such luck. All she saw in the mirror was flashbacks of what just happened, and they were more real than anything else she had experienced in her life.

 

"How could I be so stupid..." she murmured, slightly dazed by the speed the recurring of thoughts running through her head. Just yesterday, she had basically announced that she liked Daniel at the canteen in open, and just a moment ago, she accidentally confirmed and secured her position as his girlfriend. Normal people would think she was absolutely in love with Daniel.

 

And oh my God, how the thought stung.

 

It was such a bad idea digging this path. Why hadn't she backed down when she had learned Daniel harbored some sort of romantic fondness towards her? No, she just had to do the exact opposite. Stupid pride and decisiveness. If only she had seen this...

 

Could things still be rewindable? Daniel's face had contorted with nothingness but exhilaration when they won that game--how could she explain to him? How could she disappoint him? Once he learned about her real identity, hatred was his only definite route.

 

She bombed it. She had truly bombed it. To think that the reason why she was even in this pithole in the first place was to see if she should unite with her family or not. But now even if she wanted to, she couldn't anymore. Family reunion had never seemed so bleak.

 

Gillian wiped her face with a towel. She didn't know what to do next. Outside, Daniel was probably still jumping in glee. Truthfully, she didn't want to leave the bathroom. She didn't want to confront Daniel, or anybody else for that matter. If only there was a way she could live in this bathroom and survive solely on water.

 

Pretty barbaric but the thought of isolation soothed her.

 

Suddenly, the door banged open. Its abruptness was harsh and made Gillian jumped with fret.

 

"Scheming your next move?" The edgy voice of Edison Chen sneered.

 

No, not him again. She had enough of his interrogations. Why couldn't he give her a break? Couldn't he see that she was just as flustered as he was? But wait, he probably thought she was snickering inside with total mirth.

 

"Did you not see the sign outside or are you too blind? The men's room is next door if you haven't noticed." Gillian coldly replied.

 

Edison slammed the door shut and grabbed the mop nearby to lock it. For a moment, she wondered if he had thought about hitting her. Surely, he must have been frustrated at her to that scope at one point... She could say the same for herself.

 

"Playing the cold card, are you? Funny, you were anything but cold back out there." Edison crossed his arms.

 

Actually he was wrong. She had been stiff ever since she made that final shot. Perhaps he was too blinded by his own anger and Daniel's exuberance to have noticed that. "And since when have you became the keen observer?"

 

"Oh shut your crap up! What did you think you were doing?" Edison asked, finally hissing. "I can't believe you would do that! He is your damned fucking brother for crying out loud!"

 

"Don't you think I know that?" Gillian asked.

 

"Apparently, you need reminders!" Edison shouted. "To think that I trusted you enough to know what you're doing. But now, I really, really doubt that. I can't believe I didn't fucking bust you out. What the hell was I thinking all of those past weeks?"

 

His insult slapped her. It was quite commendable how he kept his words about protecting her secret all the while, trying to maintain loyalty to his friend. Yet, that commendable trait threw daggers at her own dignity. Somehow, it made her feel like a bad person. "I don't need your reminders."

 

"You're such a scum. I have never met such a conniving, evil bitch like you. I'm not going to play your fucking games anymore. Daniel is my friend, and I won't stand by to see him played like a fool. I'm going to tell--"

 

"Do you think I really want this to happen?!" Gillian cut through him, her patience had totally evaporated.

 

Edison gazed at her, stunned. Obviously he hadn't expected such a response. "Oh, so what? You actually had another plan?"

 

"Is that all you think of me? A scheming bitch waiting to execute her plans at any minute possible?"

 

He scoffed. "What else? I can't possibly think you're a princess?"

 

"You really think I would ruin my own brother?" Gillian asked, her tone sharp.

 

"Why wouldn't you? I mean, he is so fabulously rich! Just a scoop from his bag and you can live the rest of your life worry-free! Your own father even abandoned you! If you're not here for revenge, than what else could it be?"

 

Gillian's eyes hardened. The mention of her father seemed to do the trick. "How do you know I was abandoned?"

 

"Come on! Everybody knows what type of person your mother was!" Edison shrilled. "When she cheated on your father, it was on the headlines for weeks! She totally brought a bad name to the Wu's. It is no wonder that your own father didn't want you anymore."

 

Her fists curled as a whip of revulsion spanked her mind. No, she did not know any of this, but this guy, how could he know so much? Things that she didn't even know? Did he made all of that trash up? "You're lying!"

 

"I don't have the time to fucking lie to you. Go ask Daniel if you'd like. I mean, he is so in love with you that he'd even tell you where his good spots are so you could  please the shit out of him--"

 

SLAP!

 

Edison's face snapped to the side.

 

"You fucking bastard," seethed an angry Gillian.

 

The hurt in her eyes was so evident that it baffled him. His words were a little bit out of hand, but a person like her shouldn't get so offended by such things. Unless...

 

"What have I ever done to you?" she asked, her eyes tearing with wetness. Edison was so stunned by her faltering of strength. She had sounded and acted so tough that he never considered a girly side of her possible, but now, she resembled a little girl, lost and hurt. "Why do you always insult me? What did I do to you?"

 

Edison's mouth remained inadvertent agape. The way she was responding was obviously too perplex for him to grasp. They were just words! Why was she so overworked about them? And it wasn't like she was an innocent girl. His "insults" did hold some truth in them. So why?

 

"Ever since I met you, you never cease to make my life a fucking hell. You think I like pulling a bitchy face whenever I confront you?" Gillian's voice was shaky with overwhealmed emotions. "You think I like being called a manipulative bitch every single time? What do you think I am? I have feelings too, you know! I'm a human being. Not a witch!"

 

He stood still in his posture, but he couldn't utter a word. It was true. A manipulative bitch was all he ever saw in her and he never stopped twice to think about it. Now, the way she was breaking down brought a whole new impression. She, too, was a girl... in need of protection.

 

"I'm not here to ruin my brother, to inherit his riches, or whatever." She continued, tone still croaking. But there was no sight of tears running down her cheeks. They were all glittering in her eyes. Even when she seemed like a girl, she was a stubborn one. "I just wanted to learn more about him. I want to be myself when I'm around with him. If he knew I was the sister that was abandoned, would he still treat me as one? I mean, you said it, I was abandoned. Why would they take me back after all of those years? I was probably a shame to them."

 

Gillian turned to face him directly in the eyes. He could see everything in there. The sincerity, the insecurity, fear, anger, all ebbed in those dark brown pupiles. For once, guilt rambled every part of his body. "If I just came up to him and said I was his sister, how would he react? He'd think I have some interior motives. He wouldn't believe me. Even if he did, he probably won't like me. Who knows what he had heard about me? So why would I take the risk of being rejected again when I can be accepted as an entirely new person?"

 

Edison could feel his hate for her faltering, but his pride wasn't willing to let that go easily. "You think I believe in that crap?" His mouth spoke without his consent. "I mean, if that was true, you wouldn't deliberately try to seduce--"

 

"Seduce? Is still goes back to that huh? I'm just a seductive, manipulative, evil bitch to you no matter what, is that right?"

 

"Well--" he stopped, not knowing what to say to that.

 

"Why am I even talking to you? No matter what I say, you won't believe me." Gillian said flatly. Her emotional side disappeared and what surfaced again was her usual demeanor. The transformation was so brief that Edison wouldn't believe she was the same person if he hadn't witnessed her breakdown. "All you want to hear is, I’m Daniel’s sister and I came here to purposely ruin him."

 

CLUMP.

 

Edison and Gillian immediately looked towards the door. Someone's purse lay on the ground, and undeniably, the look on the shocked Ruby's face told that she had heard something.

 

Horror climbed onto Gillian's expression. How could this be? It was a Thursday evening and no way could Ruby be here now unless she had extra classes--

 

"Oh my gosh!" Ruby finally recovered from her initial shock and exclaimed. "I knew it! There was something fishy about you all along! I so knew it!"

 

"Rubes, keep it down!" Edison hushed, fearing that the others might hear. They were just outside in the basketball court, and the restrooms weren't so far away.

 

"Edison! Did you hear her?" Ruby asked, her eyes wide as though the revelation will never get old. "She's... she's Daniel's sister, the Wu Yan Tung! The tramp's daughter!"

 

Gillian put herself on defense mode as she glared at Ruby. "Don't call my mother a tramp."

 

"It's really her!!" Ruby dashed over to Edison and grabbed his arm. Apparently, she didn't consider the notion that he might have already known before exclaiming, "Edison, can you believe it? She must have come back to get revenge!"

 

"Are you done?" Gillian hissed, her fists curling at Ruby's disrespectful assumptions.

 

"No wonder Daniel is crazy about you. You've tricked him into your charms!" Ruby relentlessly spat, her surprise still did not diminish completely.

 

"Ruby, shut up." Edison snarled.

 

"I... I have to tell Daniel!" Ruby declared.

 

"You wouldn't dare." Gillian stopped her.

 

"Why wouldn't I?" Ruby asked. "You dirty little brat, how dare you try to prey on your own--"

 

"It's my business. You have no right to interfere--"

 

"--brother! You are one disgusting little wrench I've ever seen. First, you stole my boyfriend. Now this! I will never forgive you!"

 

"I did not steal your boyfriend-"

 

"What's going on here?"

 

Silence immediately chopped the noise into nil at the familiar voice they all knew too well.

 

No...

 

Terror swallowed both Edison and Gillian as their hearts thrashed at a much faster pace. Daniel, Takeshi and Kenny stood at the door, baffled and curious as to what was happening.

 

"Catfight in the ladies' room?" Takeshi asked, the sheer idea bringing amusement to his mind. "Edison, Edison! How do you ever do it? Get two girls to fall madly in love with you? Care to teach me?"

 

"Teach you my foot," Edison hissed.

 

"Dan!" Ruby shrieked, rushing over to his side like a puppy. She shot Gillian a triumphant look that leaked with pounds of arrogance. "You won't believe it!"

 

Panic scathed Gillian, sending adrenaline to pump throughout her body. "No, don't--"

 

"Gillian. She's Wu Yan Tung. Wu Yan Tung. Your sister!!!"

 

Edison stifled a groan. Kenny let out a gasp while Takeshi an "Oh my..." Daniel on the other hand looked completely stumped.

 

No, no, no. This was not happening! No! She was not prepared. It was all too soon!

 

Daniel's eyes fixed on Gillian. She couldn't decipher what he was thinking, but the expression on his face seemed to beg for a confirmation.

 

"You're... my.... sister?"

 

The question was so simple, so direct. All she needed to do was to answer yes or no. But damn it if it wasn't hard for her. She couldn't open her mouth, not when his eyes were boring deep into hers. Eyes that once held copious amount of glee were turning into pits of chagrin and disillusion.

 

Ruby watched Gillian with tons of contempt, her mouth stood edgy and prepared to dish out some more fuel to the fire. "I heard her! She said she was your sister and that she planned to ruin you by making you fall in love with her! Now if that isn't the most outrageous thing I've ever heard!"

 

The second blow seemed to have torn a piece of Daniel. His pained expression gave no way for Gillian's guilt to escape. "Is that true?" he asked softly, as though his voice had been severely damaged.

 

Gillian was stumped. Inside, there were so many words she wanted to say, but to convey them all... was not something her disrupted mind could do. "I..."

 

"No surprise," the indifferent Kenny chirped in. "I had a bad feeling about her."

 

Kenny's word put another knife through Daniel's heart. "Answer me!" He demanded heatedly, a side she never knew he possessed. She could see the last sparkle of hope that adorned his eyes. Perhaps, he was wishing that she'd tell him no. But God, how could she?

 

Gillian dared not open her mouth; she could only submissively nod as an image of a hammer smashed all of her dreams to pieces. This was it. There was absolutely no turning back. Daniel will hate her.

 

He gazed at her, disbelief lurking every part of his face.

 

Edison knew that he should do something to help. "Look--"

 

"I can't believe it." Daniel breathed. He was trying his hardest to be calm; everyone could see that, but his patience was crumbling into shards. "You have known it all along, haven't you?" he directed the question at Edison.

 

Edison wanted to explain, but there was no possible way out of this. It was true. He did. Right from the beginning.

 

Daniel took his quietude as a yes. Daniel let out a cold laugh as he backed away from the two of them. "You two... I can't believe it. But right, Gillian Chung Yan Tung. Wu Yan Tung… Why didn’t I see that before? I was such a fool.”

 

"Huh? Excuse me for my interruptions, but I do not get it?" Takeshi interrupted with a befuddled face. "If Gillian was his sister, then why did she agree to the conditions of the game? Since Edison lost, that means the sibs are a couple?!"

 

"WHAT?" Ruby's eyes inflated. "Edison, you competed for this girl?! How come I didn't hear about this?"

 

"You're so late, Rubes," Kenny remarked. "That game was one heck of a game and you missed it."

 

"Edison Chen! You explain to me right now!" Ruby yelled.

 

Edison couldn't help the urge to roll his eyes when it came to Ruby. "Ruby, what I do does not concern you in anyway. And have you forgotten again that I've broken up with you?"

 

"But for this brat?! I won't accept it!"

 

"You're not my mother..."

 

"Be quiet!" Daniel's voice put a stop to all bickering at once.

 

Desperation lunged in Gillian's throat, knowing that this was the perfect time to clarify the matters. Before she knew it, she grabbed his arms. "Daniel, I can explain--"

 

"No!" He yelled, throwing her hands away, his eyes a stubborn revolt. He will not let her manipulate him again! He may be a fool once, but not twice! "I'm not going to believe in a word you're going to say."

 

Gillian retrieved her hands, eyes turning glossy and all will to explain deteriorated instantly. No, he didn't mean it, did he? The thought tore her ruthlessly. "Dan..."

 

"Don't call me that," he seethed with repulsion racking his nerves. "I trusted you. I believed you. Hell, I loved you."

 

Gillian's heart was torn apart. The sadness that stricken his eyes made her stumble two steps. She wanted to explain to him, to tell him that she didn't mean for this to happen, to hurt him. What had she done? Now she was beginning to doubt if what she did from the beginning was right.

 

"I've never doubted you once, always standing up for you whenever necessary, and even going against my friends. I tried to please you, courted you. Never would I imagine that you'd turn out to be my sister! Why didn't you tell me?"

 

"I..."

 

Daniel looked away and Gillian's intention to respond was killed just like that. It hit her that no matter what she said, he wouldn't hear any of it. "Gillian Chung, I hope you're happy. From this instant, I hate you."

 

He walked away just like that, his disappearance tore a flesh of hers and gave her a whole dimension of emptiness. Kenny followed suit while Takeshi shook his head in pity before disappearing with them.

 

Ruby, however, wasn't inclined to leave her boyfriend alone with the tramp. "Edison, let's go."

 

He stayed rooted to the ground. "Rubes, stop it. Just go yourself."

 

A flame ignited in her eyes, both pride and dignity insulted. "Edison Chen, you're... you’re great." She seethed with resentment increasing its toll. Giving him one last look, she strode away from the restroom.

 

When all were gone, Gillian backed against the marble wall, sliding to the ground.

 

Edison placed a hand on her shoulder. Sure he wanted to tell Daniel the truth all along, however contrary to what he thought, he didn't feel any more relieved and  cured of burden. In fact, guilty was more of a reliable adjective to describe his feelings.

 

Gillian finally let the tears loose. She had expected Daniel's reactions; she had even foreseen it and imagined it. But when reality struck, the combustion of emotions was so utterly controlless and powerful--more than anything she had anticipated.

 

"Hey..." Edison murmured.

 

"Did you hear him?" she croaked, sobbing. "He hates me..."
