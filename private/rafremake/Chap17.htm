It was twelve midnight, and Gillian crept her way back into her dorm. Thankfully, Takeshi had shown her the way to sneak into the dormitory some time ago, given their usual late returns. Somehow, the thought of him brought nostalgic memories. She remembered Charlene had helped her find her way around on her first day.

 

"You're back," Edison's voice frightened her.

 

"What the hell are you doing still up at this hour?" Gillian hissed, angry that he had scared her out of her wits.

 

"Why the hell are you so scared? Shit," he scratched his head, propping onto the sofa.

 

"Would you turn on the lights?" Gillian asked.

 

"Okay, okay, sheesh, scary cat." He tugged at the lamp. "So? Why are you back today?"

 

Gillian collapsed next to him. Although the kiss had not gone forgotten in her mind, she didn't want to let it bother her. At this moment of weakness, she sought for solace anywhere, even if it meant from Edison. "It might sound crazy, but I came back for you."

 

"What?" Edison was bewildered, although somewhat thrilled. "You have got to be kidding me. You actually want to see me? Even after that kiss--"

 

"Look, forget it. We both know it meant nothing to either of us," Gillian rebuked awfully, as though the reminder did damages to her mind.

 

"Oh," Edison said stupidly. "Yeah, you're right."

 

"Of course, I'm right. You can't possibly think I'd fall for a jerk like you."

 

Edison rolled his eyes. "Please. I have women worshipping me at my feet."

 

"Stinky feet," she added.

 

Edison's face flushed. "As if yours don't stink!"

 

"They do not!"

 

"They do too!"

 

"What? You've been smelling them?"

 

"I don't have to. Your socks stink!"

 

"Whatever..." Gillian gave in and leaned back onto the sofa, letting her mind wander away. He had relaxed her, as silly as it sounded. But strangely enough, his company just opened the gates to her heart. "Daniel came to see me today..." She unleashed vaguely.

 

Edison looked at her. From her face, he discerned a quick convulsion, as if she was stung by the memory. "What did he say to you?" He prodded.

 

"He said we are no longer related," she turned to him and laughed, although it contained neither humor nor seriousness. "What a joke." She was bitter; Edison could see that.

 

"Well, at least he didn't cuss you out," Edison added in attempt to lift her spirits.

 

"That would have been better..." She blinked her eyes, and a tear dropped with significance. "At least, I wouldn't feel so bad."

 

"Oh, stop moping. It's not like he can deny you're siblings. Please. He is not God."

 

"He's not, but he has the power to reject me," Gillian said sadly but distantly. "The one thing I'm terrified of happening has happened."

 

He studied her. At that instant, a swarm of questions barged into his mind. "Why didn't you tell him the truth in the first place? If you asked me, you have your well-deserved dessert," he couldn't help but be feisty. After all, he couldn't understand her asinine decisions. But as thoughts and previous conversations scrambled to his mind, he grasped a conception. "Oh... You didn't want him to reject you."

 

"He thinks I seduced him." Gillian turned to him with that sardonic stare. "You must be happy. He is finally getting what you were trying to tell him."

 

Edison stared at her, the inexactness of her words plunging him into a state of confusion. She was right about his attempt to whack some sense into Daniel, but Edison wasn't exactly happy... There was a whole different dimension behind the story, and Edison couldn't grasp why. "Well, did you?"

 

She raised her eyebrow. "Did I?" Gillian's mystifying voice revealed her uncertainty. "Maybe I unconsciously did... Who knows?"

 

"Did you, or did you not?!" Edison was vexed at her vagueness. "It's a yes or no answer, god damn it!"

 

"You think it's a yes... so let's say it's a yes."

 

Edison was irritated. "Come on, woman, your reputation is on the line here!"

 

"As if I had a good one to begin with... What does it matter? Even my own best friend seems to have the wrong impression of me," Gillian spat back with little ambition to clarify. She was too cluttered to reproach her diminishing pride.

 

"Hah! It's that Song Hye Kyo, huh?" Edison tittered, ego soaring that he was right all along. "I knew there was something about her! From the moment I met her at the doorway..."

 

Gillian rolled her eyes. "You don't know her, so shut up."

 

"And it appears that you didn't even know her either, so I think you should shut up," Edison rebuked, trying to bonk some sense into her deteriorating brain. "So now what, you hate her?"

 

"She seems to hate me... She wouldn't even look at me the whole day," Gillian remembered Hye Kyo's coldness and became saddened at the prospect of them becoming potential enemies. She didn't want it to come true, but the uncanny part of her signaled a similar prophecy.

 

"Oh well! You shouldn't even give a speck of care to backstabbers!" His detest was palpable. "What good are you if you can't even be loyal to your friends?"

 

Gillian studied him. "Is that why you're so loyal to yours?" She asked softly.

 

"W-what?" Edison was suddenly embarrassed at the cheesiness he embarked upon himself. "Whatever! What I'm saying is, forget that backstabber! Daniel is foolish for believing what she had to say. But seriously, your only concern now is to patch up with Daniel."

 

Her eyes were unrelenting, and discomfiture drowned in on Edison at her indispensable stare. "What?"

 

"I can't believe it. I actually think Edison Chen is handsome," Gillian said, almost in a jest.

 

"What?! You mean, you never thought I was handsome before?!" He seemed outraged as her comment spanked his ego.

 

"Nope..." She giggled at his maddened expression.

The next morning, Gillian got a phone call from Jared inviting her to attend dinner that day. He even asked Edison to come along. Puzzlement drenched her the whole day as she wondered the reason why for the sudden summoning. Inside, anxiety was fueling. She couldn't help but think something dismal was befalling. Nothing pleasant could be possible at this stage.

 

Edison accompanied Gillian. At times like this, she appreciated his gesture of support. It was amazing how he turned around and offered his best. Truthfully, she couldn't unravel why he no longer bore hostility towards her. What did he see in her that made him change his mind?

 

Charlene approached Gillian when they chanced into the living room. Astonished, Gillian's eyes spot several others. Rainie, Ethan, Ruby, Takeshi, Kenny, Ariel, and even Hye Kyo were all present. What did this mean? Why did Jared summon everyone? "Hi Gillian. How are you?" Charlene asked genuinely.

 

Charlene's concern touched Gillian's heart. "I am alright, thanks."

 

"That's good to hear. But really, if you want to bawl your eyes out, I'd be glad to be a handkerchief."

 

"I appreciate that. Though, I wouldn't want to dirty you." Gillian laughed lightly. It was the first real laughter in days.

 

From the side, Rainie was restless. She wanted to talk to Gillian, but after her identity was revealed, Rainie was forced to reproach her feelings toward the lass. Even though she believed there was no way Gillian could be that manipulative, she had to be cynical. For her family's sake, she had to.

 

Ruby crossed her arms in an aloof manner, somewhat irritated by Charlene's display of an intact friendship. "Charlene, you should know better not to talk to dishonest people."

 

"I think that's my call, isn't it?" Charlene replied with a tinge of distant annoyance. "I think what Gillian did was understandable."

 

"Understandable?" Ruby's eyes widened. "Don't humor me. She seduced her own brother. That is beyond evil in my books."

 

"As if ostracizing someone isn't evil."

 

"Charlene!" Ruby couldn't believe her ears. "What are you doing?"

 

"I'm doing what my heart thinks is right," Charlene amended. "Gillian was only trying to get to know her brother. It was out of her wishes that things happened this way. If you have forgotten, Daniel was the one who went after her first."

 

"Ridiculous! She was the one who tempted him first--"

 

"Exercise your brain a little bit," Charlene snarkily suggested. "Everything had been Daniel's initiative."

 

"I miss the old days," Takeshi lamented. "Where is the love? Where is the fleeting happiness of endless friendships?"

 

"Shut your cheesy mouth up," Kenny barracked from the side.

 

Takeshi sighed. "It's not my fault..."

 

Takeshi's statement caused a strip of guilt to shame on Gillian once more. Everyone's misery was her doing, she fully understood that. Even so, she hadn't expected Takeshi's honesty. It was like a spank across her mind, one that stung terribly.

 

"Pft. I don't see what's so bad about the situation now. At least, we now know we can't trust somebody," Ruby said with a sass. "If you ask me, I say everything is perfect the way it is now."

 

"You call the rift between Edison and Daniel perfect?" Charlene queried with a denigrating tone.

 

"I mean," Ruby was caught off guard. "You know what I meant!"

 

"You're just happy Gillian is an outcast now," Ariel poked in angrily.

 

"Ariel... Don't butt into other's business," Hye Kyo reprimanded in a graceful tone. She always managed to appear sophisticated, and Gillian wondered how.

 

"Don't be a hypocrite, Kyo. You're the one who caused all of this," Ariel pinpointed.

 

"What? That's incredulous. Gillian was the one who lied." Hye Kyo feigned hurt.

 

"Yeah! You shouldn't roll the blame onto her. She only said the truth," Ruby backed the girl up.

 

Ariel rolled her eyes. "Truth? More like exaggeration."

 

"Please. The truth is the truth. You can't exaggerate the truth," Ruby defended.

 

"Ruby, you are the epitome of exaggeration," Edison commented.

 

"W-what?" Ruby looked alarm that Edison attacked her. "At least, I don't lie! Edison! Why are you defending Gillian? You know in your heart what she did was outrageous--"

 

"May I have your attention, please, everyone," Jared's authoritative voice put a halt to the nonstop bantering between the group of friends. Instantly, all eyes landed on him, Lily, and Daniel, who all had just come from upstairs. Daniel's expression was inexplicable, as if he had drained all of his emotions before appearing. Gillian's heart was wrought with a throb at his repugnance. "I am glad that everyone is here. Ruby and Daniel had told me the whole story, but today, I want to hear Gillian's side." Lily helped him sit down on the sofa, and everyone looked at him apprehensively.

 

Gillian fidgeted as Jared's eyes rest on her. He figured she needed prodding, so he continued, "Gillian, can you tell us why you didn't tell us in the beginning?"

 

She froze with unease. Truth be told, she was embarrassed to have her whole problem aired out in public like this, but her mind quickly reprimanded her. This was not a time to feel embarrassed! "I didn't think you will accept me," she said honestly.

 

Jared raised an eyebrow. "And why not?"

 

"Because... because you hated my mother," Gillian's voice became small.

 

"You are right about that," Jared affirmed her feelings. "But that does not transfer to you."

 

"Then why did you send me away?" Gillian asked, feeling undignified all of the sudden. "You abandoned us. She was carrying your child and you left her to sustain by herself!"

 

Jared perceived her pain to be his fault, and he faltered in his rigidness. "I am sorry, Gillian, there is a reason why I asked everyone to be here. I want to clarify some issues with everybody, because I know you dont deserve to be condoned for what you did. You are not my daughter." He said.

 

Gillian's eyes hardened. "What?"

 

"I would never imagine I have to confront this issue after so many years, but your mother and I were happily married, that is true. But we divorced within two years, on the allegations of infidelity. I sent your mother away to an estate I owed in the States, as a term for alimony."

 

Surprise dawned on every single person in the room, but it wasn't as significant as the shock that stiffened Gillian. She stared at the old man in front of her, scathed in indisposition to believe the hurting news. "Are you saying my mother cheated on you?"

 

He held her gaze. "Precisely." His usual kindness resurfaced at the pain in her expression. "I am sorry it has caused you so much grief. Apparently, your mother was not a virtuous woman to have bred you into an embittered person."

 

"Please don't smear my mother," Gillian reproached his statement. "Even what you said was the truth, Daniel is still my half-brother, is that correct?"

 

Jared closed his eyes.

 

"You cheated on her, didn't you?" Gillian suddenly understood his silence.

 

"Did it not seem odd to you that you and Daniel are of the same age?" Jared asked. "There is no way you two could be related."

 

Daniel suddenly restored facial vitality as the news unlocked a rigid gate in his heart. "Dad, you mean Gillian is not my sister?"

 

"She could have been," Jared said vaguely.

 

"Damn it, dad. Say it clearly!"

 

"Were you not listening?" Jared stared at his unwise son. "Your mom died a long time ago. Gillians mother has no relation to you whatsoever."

 

Gillian was unfettered with agony. Although she felt somewhat relieved, nothing felt more terrible than knowing you had no family. All this while, what she fetched after was an empty dream. Gillian felt like she had been deflated and stripped of purpose, banishing her into a state of despondency. "I am such a fool..." She muttered, broken.

 

Jared looked at her, pity dwelling in his old eyes. "I am sorry, Gillian. I would never imagine how much you wanted to be a part of our family... Your mother is so indecent to give you a false dream."

 

"Like I said before, please don't smear my mother!" Gillian yelled, breaking down. She was upset. Mad at the fact that everything had turned out unexpectedly. "You had a fault in this since you cheated on her too."

 

"I know. I am aware that it was not mature of me to punish her back," Jared looked terribly stricken at his insensitivity. "But Gillian, be honest. Were you trying to take part in the inheritance?"

 

"No!" Gillian blared offensively, not believing the outrage. "Not everyone is after your money!"

 

"Gillian..." Daniel was uncomfortable to see her so angry.

 

"Don't," she stopped him from offering her words of assurance and pity. "You all may think I am lying, but money wasn't what I was after! I really thought you were my brother.... and that I had a whole family in this place. How foolish. How absolutely stupid of me, to wallow in this imagination! I really wanted to be your sister..." She suddenly cried, the tumult putting her into anguish zone as pricks of misery pierced her. "You don't know what it's like to be alone in this world..."

 

Daniel had the urge to hug her, but the coldness she emitted signaled she didn't want his comfort. "I understand, Gillian..."

 

"No, you don't," she bluntly said, maddened at the fact he tried to sympathize with something he had nil experience of; "You never went to school and had kids make fun of you because you didn't have a father. You never grew up, wondering what was wrong with you, to have you father abandon you without a single letter of assurance. You never saw your mother die in wretchedness, hoping for a last chance to see a man she loved. You don't understand what it's like!" Tears rushed to her face as the surge of emotions over empowered her aloofness.

 

Everyone looked at her with sympathy, not knowing what to offer her in her times of levitating disappointment. Jared rescued everyone from their helpless situation. "Gillian. Years have passed. There is no use in mourning. We can all be family."

 

Gillian looked up at him. "Family?"

 

"I can see that you and Daniel are in love. It is happy news that you're not related, am I right," Jared said with fatherly affection, blessing the conversation with welcoming warmth. "I have absolutely no objection to you two dating."

 

Daniel's face erupted in vivacity, head spinning in one exact emotion. "Gillian!" With strong arms, he embraced her as though she was a little child. Gillian's mind had evaporated in logic, defeating the misery that had enveloped her. "I am so glad."

 

Although Gillian's sadness had diminished a bit at Jared's proclamation that they were family, she couldnt decipher what she was feeling exactly. She liked Daniel, but she felt something was terribly wrong when her eyes made contact with Edison's.
