The Rich & the Fabulous: The Remake

by Bunny Ears

Chapter Three

 

The next Sunday morning, Gillian woke up with slight exhaustion. She never knew it was so hard to fall asleep on a strange bed but then she knew that it wasn't the cause of the restless night.

 

No, it wasn't. If anything, it had to be him.

 

Gillian had to admit, she was a bit fazed out. She hadn't planned to see him so soon, but since she had found him, she might as well further onto her plan. Tomorrow, she'd had to take care of a few things at the University and register a dorm, and those kinds of things. She knew Daniel went to that school, so if she met him again tomorrow, it would be a great chance to get closer.

 

It sounded almost malice. But really, it wasn't. Gillian bore no harmful intentions. She just wanted to find out more about her family whom she heard of for all of her life but never really had any relationship with other than sharing the same blood.

 

Gillian bit her lips. Her mom had just passed away almost a year ago. She had mailed her father in HK of the news, but still she hadn't gotten a reply. Now she was beginning to doubt if he really cared at all. Why didn't he look for her? Why didn't he even send a response to at least inform her that he had gotten her news? His irresponsibility was why she was here--to find the truth. She will dig into the matter and seek why her mother was sent all the way to the States and why he had abandoned her--his daughter--like this once and for all.

 

This past year, ambiguity had torn her turbulently. Being all alone was something Gillian had never thought could be so terrifying. She was certainly grateful that she had friends to comfort her in her most miserable times. Despite it being almost a year, Gillian knew that before she found the truth, the obscurity will always haunt her.

 

Which was way she refused to let Daniel knew about her identity. She wanted to investigate on this matter without her father's side of the family knowing. Of course she wanted to unite with her father but some things just had to be clear first. Once she found out what really happened, she'd decide whether or not to reunite with them.

 

Reunite...

 

As if they were ever united.

 

***************

 

 

"Thank you, George. Without you, I don't know what my Gil would do," her aunt, Margaret said with unfaltering gladness as she stared graciously into the eyes of the headmaster of the university.

 

"It was my pleasure," he replied admirably. Gillian could tell from the way his eyes glistened that he had an inkling of fondness toward her aunt and she couldn't help but feel a little giggle itching at her throat. She knew better, though, and kept quiet as the adults continued talking.

 

"You know, it's like three weeks into the semester and if it wouldn't for you, Gil would probably have to delay her plans and not have any classes until next semester." Margaret continued, "Don't forget what a big favor he's done for you, Gil."

 

"Don't worry, how can I ever?" Gillian said and winked. She was very grateful for his help indeed but a part of her didn't like the idea that she only gained the benefit of the doubt because her aunt had connections. It was a disturbing fact but she would have to live with it. She couldn't just throw her dreams into the trashcan because of her pride. And hell, if she didn't have a load of that...

 

"Why, no need to thank me. Gillian is truly a gifted student. It is an honor to have her here," George said with a sparkling smile.

 

She was on her own after Margaret left and the headmaster returned to his duties. Class hadn't started yet since it was only seven in the morning. That granted her about an hour or so to sort through her things and prepare. Considering she was three weeks late in her courses, she would have to work extra hard to make up for the time loss and shoot the exams with a decent mark. It was a tough task but for the perfectionist Gillian Chung, nothing was impossible.

 

"Need help?" A woman's voice sauntered to her ears. Her footsteps were loud and clear as she strolled over.

 

"Yeah. I need to find where my dorm is," Gillian replied, glad that help was on its way. She had been studying the picture map for awhile now but nothing was clicking to her mind. The university was huge so she did not want to take the risk of getting lost and then losing more time.

 

"Consider yourself lucky because I'm pretty damn sure I know where everything is," the girl said with a devilish smile. Her spunk personality amused Gillian in a way it made her think that that they could grow to be good friends. When Gillian told her the dormitory number, the girl's eyes shone with a spark of a laser. "Oh, you're really, really lucky."   

"How come I have a feeling that was sarcastic?" Gillian said with feigned dread.   

The girl's laughter was loud. "Well, I don't mean to scare you or anything but it seems like you're going to be stuck with a bunch of blokes."   

"You know, a clearer explanation would be appreciated," Gillian urged. Although they only talked for two minutes, Gillian felt a close connection toward the girl.   

"The blokes there are nothing but rich kids who got accepted because of their money," the girl said simply. "Enough said."   

Gillian laughed at her display of negative affection. "It seems like they're pretty hard to get along with."  

"That's an understatement, dear," she drawled. "But don't worry, I'll back you up. Anyway, I'm Charlene. Pleasure to meet you."  

"Gillian." She shot a mischievous smile. "Now, mind telling me more about this place? I could use a few tips or two."

 

"Clever, aren't you? I like."

 

They shared a laugh at that as they both embarked on the journey to Gillian's dormitory. All the overwhelming tension she felt earlier was quickly dispensing as she submerged into a comfortable zone. It was nice to know that she had a friend here already.

 

So the two went on ahead in the quest of dorm seeking. It only took them about ten minutes when they reached their destination.

 

"See, what'd I tell you? I know this place better than the back of my hand," Charlene boasted with a smile.

 

"Right, and should I present you with an award?" Gillian chuckled.

 

"I won't reject it."

 

Both girls smiled before they entered the dorm. Instantly, the sight of a messy dog nest greeted their sight. Bras, underwear, and dirty clothes were all strewn over the floor as though they were discarded trash. Papers, candy wrappers, chip bags, contributed to the horrendous sight. Gillian thought she had a heart attack.

 

"Who the hell walked into my room without any damn manners?" A crude voice pitched throughout the room. Within a few seconds, Gillian saw a girl emerging from the bathroom.

 

"Uh hi," Gillian said awkwardly.

 

"Who are you?"

 

"Relax your muscle Rubes. She's your new roommate," Charlene said.

 

"Roommate? How come I didn't get notified of anything like it?" Ruby sneered as she hopped onto her bed. The bunch of snacks and junk food got scattered to the ground from the force of her bounce. Well, there went some more trash. Not that it made any more difference than it was already.

 

Just plain disgusting.

 

Gillian decided not to be fond of this girl.

 

"Well if you actually read the letters you get, maybe you would had a slight idea," Charlene retorted with a roll of her eyes. "Girl, what's it with you? You're so clean when you're around the guys, but behind closed doors, you are a walking disaster!"

 

"You're the one to talk?" Ruby hissed. "If you hadn't moved out, I wouldn't end up this way!"

 

"Ah you finally figured out why I did!" Charlene rebuked, laughing. Ruby shot her a glare in response and Charlene only stopped when she realized Gillian was standing there feeling out of place. "Oh, right. She's Gillian."

 

Ruby approached Gillian. "Gillian?" She repeated the name. Gillian wasn't sure, but was there some sort of taunt ebbed in her tone? "Are you serious? That's such a geeky name!" She burst into guffaws as though she had no respect or manners. In fact, she didn't. "Oh my god! How do you ever introduce yourself to people? Well, it's okay. I'm going to help you. I'll give you the name Kayla. You definitely look like a Kayla."

 

Gillian's eyes bore into the laughing girl. Who the hell did she think she was? Some queen of the world? Ruby seemed like someone who needed a shove from the tip of the mountain. "Actually, I think you're the one who needs some serious change. And no, I'm not talking about your damn name Ruby."

 

Ruby's laughter died instantly. Snap. Just like that. A cloud hovered her face. "What?"

 

Gillian grabbed her luggage and dragged it across the room, towards the closet. Without bearing any heed to the blackened expression on Ruby's face, Gillian slid the closet open and pushed all of Ruby's clothes to one half of the closet. "That half is yours, this half is mine. Even though I don't have as many clothes as you do, I still expect my end of the closet stay intact from any fiber and germ of yours."

 

Charlene watched with amusement as a meek smile tugged at the end of her lips.

 

Gillian wasn't finished. Oh no, she wasn't. She trotted over towards the bed and grabbed the whole bunch of bras and underwears. With a quick action that was impossible to foresee, she shoved the pile into Ruby's face without mercy. "You like being dirty? Sure, be my guest. Just keep it out of my face." Gillian said.

 

Ruby's face reddened as exasperation took on the escalator to hell. "Bitch! Who do you think you are? This is my territory!"

 

"Uh sorry, did you not get the notice? I'm your new roommate," Gillian replied rolling her eyes in a derogatory manner.

 

Charlene laughed. "Oh Gillie, you're funny. I can call you Gillie right?"

 

Gillian smiled. "Of course."

 

Ruby shot up from her bed and stomped towards Gillian like an angry chimpanzee. "I don't know who the hell you think you are, but my name is Ruby Lin. Ruby Lin! The campus belle!" She hollered with the loudest voice she could muster. "The girlfriend of Edison Chen!"

 

Gillian's ear perked up at the familiar name. Oh, so she was that moron's girlfriend? No surprise. People from the same feather tend to stick together. "I don't really--"

 

"You heard me!" Ruby bawled like a ruthless beast. This little brat just thought she could have whatever she liked? Hah! She obviously didn't know who she was messing with. Being an irascible and unforgiving girl that she was, Ruby would never let her scamper off scot-free. "If you don't want pounds of tomatoes being tossed at you the next morning, you have better apologize!"

 

Okay, so maybe Gillian did go a bit overboard, but it was not entirely her fault was it? Gillian hadn't expected the girl to be so fired up. But she should have known really. Apparently, her temper was on nil tolerance the way Edison's temper was.

 

"Hellllloooooo!? I'm talking to you!" Ruby demanded, arching her perfectly shaped eyebrows. "Does your mother even taught you any manners? Because apparently, you don't have any! Has she ever taught you to answer people when they asked you questions? That you don't touch things that aren't yours?"

 

Ruby's insult hit so close to home. No, her mother had never spent enough time with her since she was as little as a toddler. Everything was always about work back there that there was barely any space to breathe. Nonetheless, Gillian's admiration for her mother could not be measured and the degradation in Ruby's tone towards her and her mother was something outrageously could not be tolerated. "Are you done?" Gillian hissed, finally unable to bear Ruby's harsh lashing.

 

"No!" Ruby shot back. "In fact, I want to ask you if you were slow in the brain region. You were acting as if you were mute! Like hello, speak up!"

 

"Come on girls," Charlene intervened at last, sensing the arising trouble. "You just met. Be nice."

 

"Being nice to a disrespectful girl isn't apart of my dictionary!" Ruby blared back, crossing her arms. No way was she going to be the one to initiate benevolence.

 

"Ruby Lin actually had a dictionary?" Charlene asked, laughing which earned her a deadly glare from her friend. "Okay, but still, come on. My ears are bleeding to death. Spare me some mercy yah?"

 

"Oh! Don't bequeath me! Ask her!" Ruby hissed.

 

"Uh, I think you meant beseech," Gillian spoke up.

 

"Excuse her. Her dictionary is off today," Charlene said.

 

"No. It's just lame."

 

Ruby's nose flared like a dragon as the two ignored her as though she didn't even exist. "You know what? You two go and ahead and continue your damn geek talk! I'm going out!" With that, she left the dorm with a slam.

 

Charlene let out a relieved sigh as though an earthquake had just ceased. Gillian gazed at Charlene with eyes full of curiosity. "Yes?"

 

"Why did you help me?" Gillian asked.

 

"I'm always a helper."

 

Gillian let out a laugh. "Then why didn’t you help Ruby instead?”

 

“Let’s just say, I’m pretty picky when it comes to who I help.” Charlene grinned.
