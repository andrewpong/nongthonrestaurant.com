"Gillian, wake up!" Daniel shook her drenched body, desperate to do anything to resurrect her. She was lying on the sand, limp, pale, and swollen from the water she'd swallowed. Edison stood, not too far away, uncertain of what to do. Daniel wouldn't let him near her anyway. Even Ruby, who was clinging onto Edison's arm, looked worried.

 

Gillian slurred, letting out a small moan before her eyes slowly make their appearances from her lids. "Shit, am I dead?"

 

Relief and a sudden exhilaration soared through Daniel's body like unstoppable waves, emitting his palpable emotions to the world. "Thank God!" He cried, pulling her into a hug. "I was worried sick! Gillian, thank goodness you're alive!" It almost sounded like a wail.

 

Astound petrified Gillian upon Daniel's intimate gesture. She could not discern what emotions were scathing her, but her heart skipped a beat.

 

Edison's eyes flinched. It wasn't disgusting for a brother to hug his sister, but Daniel's obvious romantic feelings only made the situation obscene. "Hey, hey, hey," he tugged on Daniel's shoulder. "Watch it."

 

Daniel glared at Edison. "Don't touch me, Ed. It was your fault that Gillian fell into the water!"

 

"My fault?" Edison looked outraged. "I'm not the one who is committing the grossest sin in history!"

 

"What are you talking about?" Daniel hissed, not understanding Edison's accusation. "You threw pebbles at us!"

 

"Yeah! To stop you from making the stupidest mistake in your life!"

 

"What the fuck are you talking about," Daniel seethed. By now, he had already stood up on his feet. His disheveled, wet hair only made his appearance more sanguine. "How dare you. Gillian is not to be insulted like that. How low of you."

 

"Low? I'm not the fool here!" Edison blurted.

 

Worry seeped into Gillian. She didn't mean for the two friends to fight each other. She hated Edison, but it didn't mean she wanted Daniel to despise him. "Look, Daniel. I'm fine. Let's just forget about it, okay?"

 

Ruby shuddered with distaste. "Gosh, tramp!" Both Edison and Daniel were her friends, and she couldn't stand to see them slug at each other because of an unworthy girl. "I can't stand it anymore! First you tried to steal my boyfriend, and now you're laying your claws on our dear Daniel. How dare you! I need to teach you a lesson---"

 

"Look, I am not going to fight you," Gillian intersected. "Let's all drop this."

 

"Excuse me?" Ruby blared. "I'm sorry, but who made you the dictator here?" Vexation was steaming through her body that it was hard to control. But suddenly, she remembered that if she busted Gillian, she could no longer be with Edison. Upon the realization, Ruby's fire died down. "Shit..."

 

"Damn it, Ruby. Leave Gillian alone!" Daniel came to Gillian's defense. "You two have no shame! Fancy trouncing on a helpless girl like Gillian. I will not stand for that!" Turning to the stiffened Gillian, his facial expression softened to that of a sweet boyfriend. "Come on Gillian. My house is nearby. Let's get you cleaned up."

 

"Hold up, here!" Ruby screamed. "I'm not finished!"

 

"Rubes, shut up!" Edison admonished her, irritated and tired of it all. He was so exhausted that his mind failed to think straight. But one thing he was sure was that he didn't want to cross Daniel any further. "Let's go home."

 

"But--"

 

"I said, let's go home!" Edison's reprimanding tone made Ruby shrink like a puppy in fear. Grabbing her wrist, he yanked her away. But Ruby turned back and glared at Gillian with ferociousness and distaste.

 

Ruby's eyes that leaked with weighty warning sizzled in Gillian's mind. But she was far too bemused at what just happened. Guilt was increasingly rampant in her veins. And Daniel's hand on hers only worsened the matter. "Don't worry, Gillian. I will protect you."

 

Tears were menacing to form in her eyes as she stared at him.

 

Daniel looked stunned. "Oh my, you must have been scared out of your wits." And with a gentle gesture, he wiped away a tear.

Upon entering the Wu's manor, Daniel and Gillian were greeted with the sight of Jared and Lily, Daniel's stepmother, with a couple of other strangers that Gillian did not recognize at the dinner table. Upon seeing how well-clad they were, she suddenly felt foolish with her wet clothes and tangled damp hair.

 

"Good morning." Shyness suddenly crept into her like a sly fox. "I-I mean, good evening." Heat ascended to her face. Damn it, how embarrassing! But she couldn't help it. Even though resentment had swallowed her the last time she'd been here, that silly hope for family reunion overcame any negative feelings that she totally forgot the letter she discovered at her father's desk a month ago.

 

"Oh, hi," Lily greeted back with an uncertain tone. Daniel never brought a girl home alone. Usually it would be his group of friends, never a lone girl. "This is a surprise. Why, darling, you're wet."

 

Jared, being the formidable man that he was, smiled gently, making Gillian's breath hitch. "You are Gillian right? I remember you from the party last time."

 

The fact that he hadn't forgotten her made her stomach churn with butterflies. She felt a tinge of happiness. "I am very flattered that you remember me..." She couldn't harbor any hatred feelings at face encounter with her father. His tenderness was just too hard to ignore.

 

Daniel beamed with pride. "Yep. Dad, Mom, let me do the formal introductions. This is Gillian, my girlfriend."

 

Surprise scattered across everyone's face. Even Gillian had to look up at him with horror. What was he thinking?!? It was going way too fast for such formalities already! Besides, she was his sister for Goodness' sake!!

 

"Oh my goodness!" A bubbly voice resounded. Before Gillian could register what was happening, a girl with beautiful brown hair rushed to her like a glittery fairy. "All of us have been waiting forever for this moment! I can't believe it! Look at you! You're so cute! No wonder brother is smitten with you!!" The girl was practically bouncing up and down like a happy child. Gillian's eyes amplified. Could this girl be her sister? She glanced towards Lily. Half-sister, perhaps. She couldn't deny entrance to feelings of exuberance. Meeting your kinship was such a heartwarming experience.

 

"Rainie, stop embarrassing your family," a slightly mature guy, probably around twenty-five, whipped.

 

"But, Ethan! This is a phenomenon. Daniel has never brought a girl home!" Rainie turned back to Gillian, observing her keenly like a policewoman inspecting a criminal. But that scrutinizing look gave way to expressions of mass approval and fondness. "You are sooo cute!"

 

Gillian blushed. It wasn't a normal thing to be called cute by a girl gorgeous like Rainie. "Thanks." The aura of positive energy seemed to have chased away all of her distress away.

 

"What type of lenses do you use?" Rainie's eyes twinkled.

 

"Huh?"

 

"Your eyes are so huge! Tell me which kinds you use!"

 

Ethan chuckled from the side. "Rainie, silly. By the look on her face, she is clueless to what you are referring to. Not everyone is into the vanity and glamour that you dispose yourself to day and night."

 

Gillian's blinked, confused. "Are my eyes strange or something?"

 

Rainie giggled. "Oh, I'm jealous. Natural beauty you are, uh huh."

 

"Good job, bro," Ethan turned to Daniel, who was beaming all along. "For a moment, I genuinely believed that you swing a different way."

 

"Huh? You mean, gay?" Incredulity marred Daniel's perfectly chiseled face. "Are you kidding me? That is the most absurd thing I've heard!"

 

"Well, you can't really blame me when you are the one clinging to Edison in a very colorful but disturbing manner. Even hermits would think there is something romantic between you two."

 

A grumble escaped Daniel's throat as the reminder of Edison resurrected hard feelings. Just the mention of him made Daniel want to hurl something at the wall. "Do not say another thing."

 

Ethan raised an eyebrow, sensing something amiss. But since he wasn't the nosy type, he dismissed it. "You know, it is quite ungentlemanly and rude of you to let a lady shivering like that in her damp clothes. What if she catches a cold? You would never live to the end of it without my endless taunts."

 

"Oh darn!" Daniel immediately turned to Gillian, who was so absorbed in the good atmosphere that her face had brightened in a way he had never seen before. "How could I be so stupid! Come on, let's get you upstairs."

 

"Oh, huh? Alright." Honestly, she was way too enamored by the new people she'd just met that she really didn't care if she was going to catch a cold. "It was so nice to meet you all."

 

"It was nice to meet you too!" Rainie beamed good-naturedly.

 

"Do join us for dinner," Jared's gaily voice resonated behind the dou.

 

With Daniel leading her up to the stairs, Gillian's mind was running with endless thoughts, capturing every single minute of her first real encounters with each of the family member. They were all so sweet and tender it amazed her. Her resentment seemed to have been completely thawed in a pace too quick Gillian couldn't comprehend. Why was she so allured by their mirth? For all she knows, once they knew who she was, those feelings would be dumped in a millisecond. At the thought of that, glumness instantly took control of her heart.

 

After she was dressed in fresh clothes from Rainie, she descended down the stairs to the dining room, where she could see that everyone was still there. Daniel had already joined them and was anticipating for her company. With a voice so affectionate, he called her over, making discomfiture to slink in her clothes. For the next fifteen minutes or so, she tried to combat her nervousness, but every time she glanced towards Jared, anxiety dunked her. Inside, she was a mess.

 

Once dinner was finished, Daniel took her up into the guest room. "It's getting so late. You should stay here for a night. You can call your aunt lest she gets worried."

 

Gillian nodded, adoring his meticulousness. Oh, why was he so kind to her? If only he wasn't, she would feel so much better. "I will."

 

Silence permeated the room, but it was comfortable, not pressuring or awkward. Daniel sat on a Victorian chair that was right across from where Gillian was sitting on the made bed. The distance was small, so Gillian could still smell his slight scent.

 

"You know... back there..." Daniel began softly, clasping his hands together as he leaned with his elbows on his knees. He had used the tone of endearment towards her before, but this kind of voice that bewails a mound of inexplicable emotions stunned Gillian. She had never heard him speak so softly, almost inaudible if other sounds were present. "I was really worried about you. I--" He seemed to be struggling. Was there a hint of shyness that she detected? "I felt like my mind and body was set in free fall. I mean... I have never felt that way before. It was so... frightening." He didn't seem confident that he was using the right words. "Do you know what I'm saying?"

 

Gillian stared at him, her inside blaring with alarm. Pain and remorse tore her apart like no other. Oh how could she do this to him? What had she done? Self-hate and raging distress fulminated inside her core, suffocating her slightest will to breathe.

 

She liked Daniel so much... She was even starting to be fond of her family. In only a day, they had granted her such heartwarming feelings that she didn't have the strength to relinquish. And Daniel, how could she forget? The way he treated her the instant they met... He was like an angel, sent from heaven to rescue people from moments of distraught and bestow you feelings of love and care. She was so honored to have him as a brother... Yet it was the very same fact that tormented her.

 

Oh how selfish of her! To have kept such a big secret! Did she not foresee that it could only bring heartache and pain? How did she manage to blind herself from her foolish tactic? She failed herself so bad. And she was failing Daniel in a cruel, brute way.

 

"I..." Daniel continued, ignoring her awkward silence. His eyes peered into her glassy ones, redness flushing his cheeks. "I think I love you..."

 

Like a flash, pang gashed her. Her breaths became increasingly shallow as her mind rambled with self-deprecating thoughts, threatening to explode. How long had she foreseen this predicament? How often did she confront Daniel's pure love and refuse to address it? She was so senseless! Why didn't she try to stop feelings from blossoming to this irreparable growth? Had she lost her mind in her quest for answers?

 

Tears threatened to formulate behind her eyes as she gazed at his earnest face that was waiting for her response. She felt so terrible. "Daniel..." For the next minutes, her brain was racing for a heartfelt apology and confession. She had to tell him. She just didn't have the heart to bear this anguish anymore. "I--"

 

"I don't mean to make us rush or anything!" Daniel suddenly blurted out. "I'm so sorry! I don't mean to scare you. I am fully aware that we are going way too fast. Just forget what I said ok?"

 

"Wait, Daniel--"

 

"Gillian... You don't have to say it if you don't want to. I understand. I'm just really glad that you are safe." Daniel's earnest wish to drop the matter made Gillian shrink back in submission. "From now on, you are safe with me, okay? I will not let anything or anyone hurt you. Gillian, I want to protect you forever."

 

Her heart melted as his sweet words savaged her. She was utterly defeated. His care for her had touched her beyond words, beckoning tears to spring forth from her soulful eyes. Never in her life did someone care so much about her.

 

Daniel was taken aback by her tears. It was the second time that day. She must have been so scared when she fell, Daniel thought. Such notion only served to strengthen his resolve. "It's a promise I plan to keep." He smiled, wiping her tears away with his finger and tucking her hair away from her face.

 

Gillian faltered as she embraced him with her whole heart pouring with emotions. For that moment, she just wanted to indulge in the sanctuary Daniel provided. She didn't care if she was being selfish. "Sorry, Daniel..." she muttered quietly. If you knew who I was, you would hate me Dreadfulness crawled across her heart at such thought.

 

A small smile tugged at his lips. "It was no big deal. I am a good swimmer. Consider yourself lucky."

 

Gillian was bemused for one second before her expression gave way to jolliness. Daniel's pureness just made you want to smile.
