Chapter Nine: The Dinner Club

Everyone let out a disgusted groan at the sight of the dirty canteen.To think that this university had gained its name of elitism and classiness. The scene before them contradict every modicum of the golden title. It was six in the evening and who the hell wanted to spend the rest of their evening in this trashy place, cleaning up?

Ruby as hell didn't want that.

"This is all your fault!" Ruby shot an accusing finger at Gillian. "If it hadn't been for you, we wouldn't end up this way!"

"Come on, we all have a fault in this..." Daniel tried to defend Gillian, but Ruby would hear none of it. She was far too blinded by her hate for this girl that any other feeling was impossible to bear. "Let's start okay? Why don't Tackie clean the tables. Gillian, you can sweep the floor. Edison, you take out the garbage. Ruby; you can wash the dishes. I'll wipe the stuff in the kitchen."

"Oh, who died and made you king?" Takeshi asked in jest.

Daniel rolled his eyes. "Someone has to divide the jobs up. Do you have any other ideas?"

"No, but I didn't need anyone to tell me what to do."

"Shut up and get to work. I'm not planning to stay here all night," Edison growled.

"Aw, Edison! Don't worry. Do you want me to help you?" Ruby asked sweetly, trotting over to his side.

"Uh, I'd rather not," he replied bluntly, making her pout.

Gillian had always started working. Everyone was just too busy yapping at each other that they hadn't noticed. "See, look at Gillian. You guys should all be like her," Daniel commended her diligence and no-nonsense attitude.

Ruby rolled her eyes, thinking Gillian did that just to capture people's hearts. "What a rude bitch," she said loudly so as to make Gillian hear her perfectly.

Gillian had half the urge to swing her broom at the obnoxious girl but she restrained her demonic side. Ruby didn't worth her time. And she didn't want to cause any more unnecessary trouble than she already did. After all, she was the culprit to the whole mess and Gillian felt guilty that she had pulled the whole crowd down with her. If only she had just accepted that stupid F. But then, that would never happen given the scope of her pride.

"Look, let's just put it this way. If you guys don't wanna work, just leave it to me," Gillian said.

"Oh come on, why would we do that?" Takeshi asked, grinning. Gillian found his transformation a bit hard to believe. Just a few days ago, he didn't even give a damn about her. But now, the respect he bore was almost miraculous. "We're in this together."

"Please, she's just playing cunning. She offered to do your dirty work today; the next morning, she'd head straight to the headmaster to tattle tale on you. I know those tricks pretty damn well," Ruby cynically said. For a moment, she appeared highly intellectual, an image that rarely ever associated with Ruby Lin.

"Talking about yourself?" Edison asked mockingly, offending Ruby who always took his comments to heart.

"Stop talking. Why don't you guys ever give it a rest?" Daniel asked, annoyed.

"Oh, don't command me what to do!" Ruby rebuked, anger ascending. "It isn't my fault we got into this. If there's anyone to blame, it's that troublemaker. Sheesh, why am I always blamed for everything?" Ruby glanced around when nobody replied to her remark, only to find that they had already started working. Fuming with indignation that she was rudely ignored by everyone, Ruby stomped off to the kitchen and attend her task.

Gillian was sweeping the floor when she suddenly heard clashes of dishes shattered into pieces. Shocked by the loud noises, everyone perked their heads up to see what was going on.

"This is stupid!" Ruby complained, hating the fact that she had to carry piles of dishes to the dish washer. To demonstrate her displeasure and unwillingness to do such a degrading task, she stomped to the tables in the lounge and sat herself onto it. "I'm not doing it anymore! I can't do this all day! It wasn't my fault in the first place anyway!"

Takeshi shook his head. "Come on Rubes. You got to help out."

"But my nail!" Ruby wailed. "Look, it's broken! And it's all your fault!"

"My fault?" Takeshi pointed at his nose.

Daniel let out a sigh. Nobody will hear the end of this.

*************************

Hours passed as the crew worked to clean the canteen. But no matter how hard they cleaned, the place always seemed to grow back dirty. Was it how it supposed to be, or was it their work that was lousy?

It was eight thirty when they all decided to take a rest. Daniel, shoving his shy side for once, decided to accompany Gillian at one of the tables where she was having dinner.

"Chicken sandwich?" Daniel raised his eyes up. "Where did you get that from?"

Gillian gawked at him. Of course she was very aware of his intention to chat with her. She appreciated it, but she as hell didn't appreciate his real motive. "Unlike the rest of the people here, I was smart enough to bring some food."

Daniel laughed. The way she said it appeared funny, somehow. Well to him, anyway. "You're really cocky, you know that?"

Gillian shrugged. His slight insult wasn't meant to be one, so no offence taken. "Just say it. I'm a bitch."

He didn't deny. "Well... at least you're not unreasonable."

They both glanced at Ruby, who was bugging Edison and whining about her hunger. They smiled as though they shared a secret. "You know, you're the only one tolerable from the whole group. With Charlene that is."

Daniel's gaze remain unchanged. "Not really."

Interest sparked in Gillian's eyes. What did he mean by that? "What?"

He smiled, mischief dancing in his eyes. It was then that she realized that he had dark amber eyes, just like herself. "Well, I have my bad points too, you know? If you really know me, you'd think I'm intolerable too."

She studied him. "So, you're saying you're acting fake around me?"

"No!" Daniel retorted almost immediately, as if insulted. He didn't realize she was going to take his words the wrong way. "I mean... Well, we haven't really gotten to know each other yet."

Yeah, that was right. Still, she couldn't find any unlikable traits about him. He was so nice, so kind, so affectionate, loyal, sweet, that typical boy next door, only just much, much more adorable. Compared to Edison, oh my god, that was like comparing a cockroach to a phoenix. Cockroach. Gillian laughed mentally at that. Boy, that sure fit him! Annoying and useless.

Her funny face made him stare at her. Apparently, she was smiling about something. "Look, I mean it. I'm not fake around you. This is the real me. I swear. If you know me enough, you'll see this is the real me, along with my bad points. I'm not lying."

Gillian blinked. He seemed so uptight to make himself clear to her, which made her slightly guilty. Boy, this was a problem that she would never get herself out of. "Hey..." She put a halt to his jabbering that would have been endless if she had not interrupted. "Um, want some?" she offered her sandwich, not knowing how to respond.

Daniel gazed at her weirdly. But of course, not wanting to make her feel bad, he accepted it and she broke half a piece of her egg sandwich.

Suddenly, Takeshi appeared out of no where in his sweaty attire resulted from the endless scrubbing. "You guys have food here?! And you didn't share?!" Takeshi looked outraged. "Dan! I thought I was your good pal!"

Daniel rolled his eyes, hating the fact that his friend just interrupted his time with Gillian. "Get lost."

"So evil." Takeshi situated himself next to Daniel. "Look at you two. Eating from a sandwich. How romantic!"

Disturbance instantly flickered itself onto Gillian's expression, perhaps even horror. Time and time again, that same thought always made her brew in guilt and disgust. Daniel seemed to notice her awkwardness and immediately jabbed Takeshi's stomach with his elbow. "Quit it!"

"Ow!"

"It will be a scream from you next time," Daniel quietly threatened.

"I know I have like the perfect voice, but you don't have to hurt me to hear it," Takeshi pouted, receiving a deadly glare. "You're no fun. I'm leaving."

"Edison! Come back!" Ruby's voice could be heard. Both Daniel and Gillian turned to where it came from and saw that Edison was approaching them.

He shot Gillian a hazardous glare, one that could kill. "Dan and Gill. What are you two doing? Huh?" He hissed, eyeing one half of the sandwich in Gillian's hands to the other half in Daniel's, suspicion caroused. However, Daniel mistaken that for jealousy.

"Oh this? We're just sharing a sandwich. I'd like to give you some, but you know, there is just so little to offer."

Edison sat himself in Takeshi's seat. "Oh man, you don't know what you're digging yourself into." He said solemnly. "I can't believe--"

"Just shut the hell up and mind your damn business," Gillian bluntly cut through before he spilled out any unwanted trash. He had done it so many times before that Gillian could never confide in him. One day, she was going to have to do some real blackmailing to stop him from ruining her plans. Apparently, the Ruby one had wore off on him.

Edison shot her a glare, forgetting that he was supposed to act friendly to her with his supposedly "courting". "Just what the fuck is your problem, witch? I don't think I was talking to you, was I?"

"Didn't you see we were having a conversation before you came here?" Gillian rebutted stubbornly.

"Uh no. You were seducing him," Edison said, emphasizing on the seducing part.

"So you do find me seductive..."

"Stop turning my insults into compliments!" Edison cried. "I swear, you are one hell of a bitch who's so up in herself."

"Same goes for you."

Daniel was confused at their constant dispute. Edison liked her, so why was he acting so hostile towards her? Come to think of it, Daniel had never seen Edison acted nice once towards Gillian. If he really liked her, wasn't he supposed to be jealous? How come what he displayed now was anything but jealousy?

Unless... That must be it. Edison must want to show his unpleasant relationship with Gillian, so as to weaken Daniel's guard. Once Daniel thinks that Edison is no longer a competition, he'll attack him right from the back and win Gillian into his hands. Inside, Edison must be want to win this competition. Of course. The competitive Ed. How could he miss that?

Edison stopped his mouth at once when he caught the increasing resentful gaze Daniel had on him. Oh damn, could Daniel have figured it all out? Edison was far too occupied with his dislike for Gillian that he had totally forgotten about his so called acting. But then again, why the hell was he doing this? Why act? Why lie to his friend for this evil girl whose mother was probably a born devil from hell? At that instant, Edison felt stupid. Just what the hell was he doing when he first lied to Daniel?

"Edison!" Ruby wailed, interrupting the slightly tense atmosphere. "Come on; we have to finish up our jobs! I don't want to stay here all night, you know!" It was obvious Ruby didn't mean any of that. Her only intention was to spend some more time with Edison. Well, bothering him, that was.

Edison tried to pluck her fingers from wrapping his arms, but she wouldn't let him so he ignored her. Okay, this was it. He will tell Daniel for sure. For everybody's sake, he had to. And he didn't give a damn to what Gillian was going to do to him. "Listen Daniel. She's a witch. God damn it, she's your sis-"

Gillian was desperate to put a halt to his outburst, but surprisingly, Daniel beat her to it. "I can't believe this, Ed." He said gravely. "I know we're courting Gillian, but man, I didn't know you were so desperate to stoop this low. Trying to smear her in front of me. I know your trick Edison."

Edison's mouth gaped open, so was Ruby's. "What? Dude, I'm your fucking friend. Why would I do--"

"Because you like Gillian." Daniel cut him off.

Ruby gasped with terrifying shock as the revelation hit her head like a hard object. "Edison, you--"

"No, I don't!" Edison blared back. "I've never liked her right from the beginning! She's all lies, I tell you. She's a damn fucking liar trying to--"

"Shut the hell up!" Daniel hissed, shooting up from his seat. His strong reaction shocked pretty much everyone, even Gillian. "Stop insulting her! I'm not falling for it!"

"Edison!" Ruby shrieked, horrified that her assumptions all along were right. "I can't believe it! You cheated on me!"

Ruby's words put another dimension to Daniel's conclusions. "I knew it! You were acting all along! Edison, you're pathetic!"

"What's going on?" Takeshi interrupted the feud. "Why are you all yelling?"

"Tackie! Edison--he cheated on me!" Ruby wailed.

"Look..." Gillian wanted to explain. Guilt was evidently scrambled across her face. This was not her intention; she hadn't meant for everything to be blown out of proportions. Perhaps, it was really time to clear up this misunderstanding. Afterall, breaking up friendships was a guilt she couldn't bear to take.

"Don't you even try!" Edison interrupted Gillian before she could say anything further that could magnify the damage. "I know what you're trying to do. Forget it! I won't allow you to-"

"Damn it. Do you want to win so badly? Why can't you take a fair competition? Is your ego that big?" Daniel interrupted.

"My ego big?" Edison asked. "Oh no, don't flatter me. If anyone's ego is big, it's his!"

"Hey, don't pull me down into this," Takeshi started, raising up both of his hands in defense. "I have nothing to do with this."

"Can't you all let me talk?" Gillian asked.

"You slut!" Ruby accused, pointing a finger at her. "You seduced my boyfriend. I knew it! Why did I ever let you go?"

Gillian tossed Ruby a glance. Okay, as far as she was concerned that cockroach's breakup with his girlfriend didn't have a thing to do with her! Ruby's insistence on her stupid hypothesis only grated Gillian. "Oh, as if you could ever do anything to me," she retorted.

"You think just because you have Daniel and Charlene, you could be cocky and do whatever you like. Arg, you don't know who you're messing with."

"Ruby, just shut up." Daniel bawled. "I don't think Gillian has anything to do with your breakup. If anything, Edison must have been the culprit to everything. I know him, man."

"She seduced you, Daniel! What makes you think she didn't seduce Edison?" Ruby barked back.

Edison's eyes amplified, apparently, the idea disgusted him. "She so did not seduce--"

"People! People!" Takeshi clapped his hands, putting a slice to the relentless flamethrowers. "Can we all just shut up and listen to me?" He asked, putting a gradual zip to everyone's mouths. He let out a relieved sigh. "Just what is the problem? Anyone care to elaborate for me?"

"Tackie! You heard it. She seduced my boyfriend!" Ruby remained stubborn with her assumption.

"Yeah, yeah, yeah, I heard that a million times," Takeshi rolled his eyes. "But what are you two fighting about?" He asked Daniel and Edison.

"We agreed to a fair competition of courting Gillian. But he's cheating," Daniel pointed out.

"I am not--"

"Hush, people, hush!" Takeshi zipped up Edison. "Okay, gosh, this is a headache..." he murmured, before turning to Gillian. He didn't like the sight of his friends fighting not even a teensy bit. "Don't you feel special?"

Gillian gazed at Takeshi. Just a moment before, he respected her; but now, it appeared as if he was mocking her. "Why would I?"

"You have all these people bickering over you. So tell me, how does it feel to be the center of attention?"

She didn't know what he was insinuating. Was he trying to say she was an attention whore?

"Tackie, it's none of your business," Daniel snapped upon sensing the underlying insult. "Gillian didn't mean for any of this--"

"Nuh uh uh uh, I'm the conflict manager here, boy!" Takeshi said. "Let's settle this once and for all. So Gillian, since these two are fighting over you, tell me, who do you like more? End this silly feud please."

Gillian was stumped. For one, she had never been forced to give out an answer like this, moreover between two people she neither wanted to go out with. One was her brother--oh god, the thought once again made her knees shaky with appall, and one was someone she'd rather die than going out with. And who did this guy think he was? Dictating what she should do and asking for answers that weren't any of his damn business?

Ruby fumed. "You had better not say Edison--"

"Rubes, Rubes!" Takeshi shot her a reprimanding glare. "Now, now, now. Nobody influence the victim's decision."

"Victim?" Ruby asked. "You have got to be fucking kidding--"

"Lighten on the language please. This is a court," Takeshi smiled as if his ridiculous scenario was all too amusing.

Edison contemplated the troubled Gillian. Naturally, of course he didn't want her to pick Daniel. They were siblings for God's sake! But then again, he didn't want her to decide on him  Hell, if he had to go out with this conniving little witch-- Wait a minute. What was this anyway? Why did she have the right to choose who and who? This was not a character picking in a game, hello! That stupid Takeshi--

Either way, Edison's friendship with Daniel was doomed. He couldn't let that witch come in between them like this. She had only known Daniel for a few weeks. Sure they were biologically related, but Edison had known Daniel since they were in diapers.

"Come on, victim. Make up your mind."

Truthfully, this was a stupid idea. Gillian didn't know what to say. If only Daniel weren't her brother, she would have blurted his name ages ago. But no, life was not that easy. Neither choices were what she wanted. But then, she hadn't planned to let her identity leak yet. There was no way that her dad, Jared, will take her back if he didn't do so earlier. And God, she'd rather be unknown than get rejected by her own kinship.

"Damn it, I can't take this anymore." Edison spoke up, glaring at Gillian as if to say, 'You're busted this time.' "Dan, you just don't know what she's fucking up to. She's trying to make you fall, dude. She's trying to inherit every dime of yours--"

Gillian immediately put a halt to his sentence. He thought he played the smart card, didn't he? Well, she'll show him. How dared he try to smear her in front of her brother! "I choose Daniel." Oh my God. Gillian gasped, not believing that she had actually let that slip.

Ruby's eyes seemed to hold some relief. Daniel's a sudden happiness while Edison's were sanguine of outrage. But Gillian... hers was of complete shock

Daniel slipped his arms around Gillian's shoulders and pulled her closer to him, as if taking her confirmation as approval. He chucked Edison a triumphant face that demonstrated all of his excitement and mirth. "Edison, you lose this time, buddy."

Gillian gazed up at the smiling face of Daniel's. His exuberance was palpable, but the only feeling she could muster was remorse.

This was a dream.

It had got to be.
