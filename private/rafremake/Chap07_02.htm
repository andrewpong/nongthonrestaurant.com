The Threat

 

 **

 

 

Gillian entered the luxurious Wu manor with utter fascination and awe. Never to her imagination that her family could be so rich. By rich, she meant incredibly two mansions, ten cars, a hundred and twenty four hotel branches kind of rich. She was so excited no words could ever be able to describe it.

 

And no, she was not caroused by the wealth, but rather by the notion that she was going to see her father once and for all.

 

How she had been craving for this day since the second she stepped into Hong Kong. True that she hadn't found out anything yet, but today, she was sure she could.

 

Gillian walked down the pathway. People would toss her judging eyes. It was no surprise. Ever since the restaurant incident, rumors had spread around that Gillian was the slut who torn up the perfect golden couple of Edison Chen and Ruby Lin. She even heard outrageous versions such as Gillian had turned Ruby lesbo that they had to do it behind Edison's back. All ridiculous. Not to mention, disgusting.

 

She didn't even want to attend this pool party. Not when the Rich & the Fab were all here. Daniel and Charlene were the only exceptions from the group that she could find tolerable and friendly--the rest, they all were blinded by Ruby's side of the story. Except for that Edison guy, who had hated her from the start. But she couldn't let them think that she was a coward.

 

No, she was not.

 

"Why the hell did you suddenly want to host a stupid pool party?" she heard Edison's voice from far away. She could also spot Charlene, Daniel, Takeshi and Kenny; all were enjoying themselves with eating snacks or sunbathing. But what really caught her attention was the fact that Ruby was nowhere in sight.

 

"Stupid? I think it's nice," Charlene quipped while she enjoyed sunbathing in the sun in her skimpy pink and yellow bikini that made her look like an Asian Barbie.

 

"You are aware you're not using sunscreen," Takeshi notified.

 

"I'm trying to get a tan."

 

"And cancer?" Edison snickered.

 

"Well, well, well, if I get cancer, then it's my problem, isn't it?" Charlene smiled.

 

"You're crazy," Edison muttered.

 

"Can I rub sunscreen on you?" Takeshi asked.

 

"Can I rub sunscreen on your lecherous eyes?" She rebutted.

 

"Then I wouldn't be handsome anymore."

 

"Gillian!" Daniel noticed her standing there and waved her over merrily. He was so happy it made her feel guilty. Sure, she wasn't killing his family, but she felt like it, being 'undercover' and all. "Hey Dan."

 

Edison's face contorted with unpleasantness at the sight of her. "Since when did you two get so close? It's Daniel for you," he sneered.

 

"Mind your business. She can call me whatever she wants," Daniel tossed Gillian a benevolent grin as he wrapped his arms around her bare shoulders. The intimacy almost made her skin crawl with goosebumps. Sure, such closeness shouldn't be a problem with siblings, but Daniel didn't know they were such; who knew what could be on his mind?

 

Edison watched Daniel with immense horror. "Get your hands off of her," he commanded.

 

Daniel tossed Edison a weird look. "Why?" Suddenly, a thought hit him. "Ohhh, you're jealous!"

 

"W-wh-sps-wh--" Edison sputtered incredulously, suddenly wanting to smack that stupid smirk off Daniel's face. But he stopped himself. Why knock his friend out for a witch? That was such a joke! Edison laughed at himself.

 

"He's crazy," Daniel whispered to Gillian delightfully, as though he was sharing a top secret with someone special.

 

Gillian meekly smiled. "Hey, you mind showing me where the bathroom is?"

 

"Sure! My pleasure!" Daniel grinned and led her into the manor. People watched them as though every movement of theirs was worth a piece of gold. Gillian felt uncomfortable. Hell, they were probably degrading her right there with their outrageous gawks. She could imagine it, hearing it at the university, that Gillian Chung tried to capture the heart of Daniel Wu, Edison Chen's best friend.

 

On their way there, there was a girl with a group of friends who sneered, "Slut." The denigrating name marred Gillian's expression, but Daniel prodded her along.

 

"I seriously don't know how these kinds of people got into my party," Daniel grunted, "They're surely a disgrace. Don't pay them any attention, okay?"

 

Gillian knew he was trying to console her. Although Gillian was pretty tough, it wasn't pretty at all when you were the target for tomato-throwing at school. She didn't even know why these people treated her so maliciously. But of course, these people were clearly just thriving on the entertainment of gossip.

 

When they reached the destination, Gillian insisted that she knew her way back and that he should return. He agreed and Gillian let out a relieved sigh. When there was nobody else in sight, she hurriedly sneaked down the hallway in the quest for a study room. Usually, that's where incoming mails were kept in drawers and all, and she was damn determined to fish out an important clue.

 

Luckily, it didn't take her long until she stumbled into a magnificent room of wall-shelves and glorious glass table. Everything looked so high-tech and beautiful she had to marvel at the whole room for a second before she indulged in searching the drawers.

 

God, she felt like a thief. But this was the only way to find out if her dad ever got her letter. If he didn't, then that explained why he didn't reply her for a whole year... but if he did...

 

Gillian let out an unwanted gasp as she spot a letter shoved deep in the corner of the drawer. Heart pounding frighteningly, she uncrumbled it and realized that indeed, that was her name and address on the envelope in the left corner.

 

So... so he had received her letter. All along, Gillian was hoping he didn't. At least, that would give her no reason to be angry.

 

But now... She had one.

 

That jerk ignored her for some decades and couldn't even bother to fly over to the States and attend her mother's funeral? Hell, he didn't even write back to tell he couldn't, or didn't want to come! All these years while her mom suffered to bring her up, he enjoyed his life as one of the richest tycoon in HK, never once asking how they were!  Did he really abandon his wife and daughter like that? In just one second, she could feel the most powerful resentment ascend to her chest like an unstoppable wave.

 

Suddenly, she heard voices. She was quick enough to hide in the corner, covering herself with the curtains.

 

"Children these days don't know what's the limit," she heard a middle-aged man spoke with tinges of disapproval. "They're all rustling the house upside down! I should have stayed in the company."

 

"Oh Jared dear," a woman spoke. "Don't stress yourself. These kids just want to have fun!"

 

Gillian peeped from her hiding place and saw them. The man must be her father. And that lady.... Gillian could feel her hands curled into fists as she remembered the step mom Daniel briefly mentioned last time. So not only did he live a luxurious life, he married another woman! So he really had forgotten about her mother! Gillian had the urge to bolt out and demand some concrete answers from him, but she restrained. She knew she couldn't do anything irrational or impulsive.

 

"Come on sweetie; let's get you some fresh air in the garden. The kids aren't there." The woman suggested. "Kids never liked gardens."

 

Both adults grinned as they dismantled out of the door. Gillian uncovered herself. She was infuriated.

 

*********

 

Gillian walked down the hallway. Her heart felt like it had hardened and she felt like chucking arrows at a target. She had come here with a heart full of excitement to see her dad, but perhaps, she should haven't been too over exuberated--now, she felt as if all of her positive energy had been drained and what was left was a bunch of bitter ones.

 

Inadvertently, her fists curled as her mind flashed to the letter she found earlier.

 

Suddenly, she felt a hand on her shoulder. Acting on reflex and instincts, Gillian immediately whirled around and smacked the attacker across his face.

 

"Ow!"

 

Gillian stared at him.

 

"What the fuck were you trying to do?!" Edison blared deafeningly, tossing Gillian an indignant look.

 

"What were you doing behind my back?" Gillian asked.

 

"I should ask you. What were you doing wandering around this hallway? The party's down there!" Edison's suspicion rose as he approached closer towards her, eyeing her up and down as if to interrogate her. She must be up to no good. What else could she be doing here? Why, that witch must be snooping around!

 

"Why should I answer you?" Gillian rebuked, bearing no intention to play the role of a criminal being interrogated by a police officer.  She was getting sick of his skepticism and tendency to interrogate her wherever she went. It was as if he couldn't go on a day without trying to pry some sort of gold from her.

 

"Hey! Stop answering my questions with another question!" Edison cried. "Tell me, what is your purpose? You were obviously trying to seduce Daniel earlier. What exactly do you want? I'm not going to stay put and watch you destroy my friend, you know."

 

Gillian wanted to roll her eyes, but then something stopped her. Edison had taken her as a born devil right from the beginning, accusing her of this and that, never once faltering on his outrageous observations and assumptions. No matter what she said, her words were not going to nullify a piece of his impression on her. Sure back then, she minded it, only because they were false... But now... she suddenly had an urge to make them true.

 

"You know what?" Gillian's eyes suddenly held a threatening glow that frightened Edison. She walked closer towards him, making him stumble a few steps backward. Eventually, she nailed him to the wall. "So what if I am?" She challenged him, her expression an indescribable acrimony. She was shorter than him by a half foot, but still, the intimidation she disposed upon Edison was undeniable.

 

Edison sputtered. "You... I knew it!!" He exclaimed. "I knew you were up to no good!"

 

Gillian let out a smile. "It took you that long to figure that out?" she taunted. "Yeah, I'm going to seduce my own brother and pave him his doom. Then, I'll inherit every dime of his. Oh, isn't that such a perfect plan?"

 

Edison's eyes amplified in disbelief. Although he felt a sudden burst of pride to know that he had been right about her along, he still couldn't contain his own skepticism. Sure, he may be a little klutzy, but he wasn't dumb. "Why do I feel that you're lying?"

 

"I am?" Gillian let out an innocent question. She neared her face towards his ear, such that her cheek brushed against his. "Well, you decide." She whispered, sending tingling down his spine.

 

Edison pushed her off, his heart a thrashing mile of beats. What the hell was she doing to him? He didn't know whether to believe her or not. The way her eyes were fluttering whimsically and mysteriously... He couldn't fathom what shuttered behind those walls. But he knew for one that if he didn't toss back a combat, he'd lose this battle. "You think you're so smart? Wait until I tell Daniel about this. Your perfect plan? You can fucking forget about it!" Edison spat harshly.

 

"You think he'll believe you?" Gillian's point hit the alarm button in Edison's mind. He could only stare at her, stupefied. "Don't make things worse than they already are. You guys are barely on friendly terms."

 

"Why are you doing this?" Edison tossed the question at her. "What's the point of getting revenge this way? Why don't you just tell him the truth and maybe his dad will give you a pity check--"

 

She slapped him. Hard.

 

Edison's head snapped to the side. "What the fuck?"

 

"Pity check? What do you take me for?" Her eyes held a fiery rage, as though offense had banished all of her coolness. But she instantly subdued her emotional side as her face crept into a mask. "Look here, we both know Daniel is smitten with me. And I know you don't want him to get hurt. So if you're smart, you'd get your nose out of my business."

 

"Or what?" Edison was indisposed to give in into empty threats. "You're going to creep into my bedroom and murder me?"

 

His taunt grated Gillian, but ire was raging inside of her at this moment. Hell, she had been raging for the past hour after the discovery of the letter. "Oh, nothing is going to happen to you. But hmm, isn't Daniel the gullible one here?" She felt kind of wrong for using Daniel, but she couldn't help it.

 

"How cheap of you to play with people's feelings," Edison said with derogatory implications.

 

"Cheap? I don't think you're qualified to even say that word," Gillian drawled, "After all, you treat women like toys and trash. If anything, you're the cheap one here."

 

"What's going on here?" Suddenly Daniel appeared from the stairs. "What are you doing to Gillian, Ed?" Daniel looked angry, as though he had assumed Edison was advancing on Gillian.

 

Gillian's eyes bore into Edison's, as if to remind him of her capability. As Edison glanced from Daniel and Gillian, the hair on his neck bristled. He would have no luck spilling the truth to Daniel. "I am having a civil conversation with Gillian, right Gillian?"

 

Gillian smiled. "Hardly."

 

Edison's lips twitched.

 

Daniel cut in between the two and pushed Edison with his palm. "Leave her alone. She doesn't like you, so why do you following her around like a lost puppy?"

 

"Don't jump to conclusions, boy," Edison replied, not believing that he was succumbing to circumstances, "She hasn't declared she likes you; therefore, I still have my chance." The words made Edison shudder with goosebumps. He would never, in reality, pursue this evil chick!

 

"Come Daniel, let's get some food," Gillian suggested, pulling Daniel away. She glanced at Edison one last time before the two went downstairs.

 

Edison was balling his fists in fury. How did he get cornered into being an accomplice to her plans? It was bewildering where he landed. If only he had burst her secret earlier, he wouldn't be in such a helpless predicament.

 

But he knew that it was no use if he were to tell Daniel. For goodness sake, the witch was right. Things were already in spoiled mode as of now; especially between Daniel and Edison.

 

****

 

Daniel and Gillian sat under a tree near the pool, gobbling on barbequed chicken. Gillian couldn't help but contemplate him from the side, finding him quite attractive. But she instantly scolded herself. How could she be admiring her own brother in such a way? It was so obscene of her!

 

But Gillian's thoughts relented when she glanced at him again, her heart softening its walls. She hadn't meant for things to go this way. Daniel was falling for her uncontrollably, and no one was to blame but herself. How could she not see it coming? From the way he treated her, his obvious attempts to get to know her better... Gillian sighed inwardly. She hadn't meant to lead her brother on, but fate had put circumstances into a blender and made a mess of her affairs. How was she going to solve this?

 

"What's wrong? Not liking the chicken?" Daniel asked.

 

"Huh? No..." Gillian hastily responded. "It's just... well... um. It sucks to see watch other people have fun in the water while I'm sitting here, ya know?"

 

"Then join them!" Glee glowed in his eyes.

 

"What? No way," she replied.

 

"What, don't tell me you're afraid of water?" Daniel asked. When she didn't respond, his pupils dilated. "No way! Seriously?"

 

"Well... Swimming class in high school just sucks okay?"

 

Daniel shot up to his feet, discarding his chicken on the paper plate. "Get up," he ordered, "I'm going to help you overcome your fear!"

 

Horror shook Gillian at the notion of coming close to the water. "Noo!" She squealed, protesting Daniel's pull. "I can't do this!"

 

"Yes you can!" He urged, "Don't be afraid. I'll be there."

 

Somehow, his assurance put a halt to her resistance. With a gentle prod, Daniel led her to the pool, where she could see Charlene sunbathing with Takeshi hovering around her, still trying to offer to rub sunscreen.

 

"Ah Gillian, it's so nice to see you deciding to get rid of that pale skin of yours," Charlene commented.

 

"I like you pale, you know..." Takeshi commented.

 

"Did I ask for your opinion?" Charlene raised her eyebrow.

 

"Come on Gillian, join us!" Daniel exclaimed, already in the water. "Just dive. It's really easy!"

 

"What if I don't come back up?" Gillian asked like a little child.

 

"Trust me, you have to!" Daniel chuckled at her innocence. "Besides, if you don't, I'll swim you back, okay?"

 

"The duck can't swim?" Takeshi gasped. "Then I'll slip into my part-time super hero job and catch you too!"

 

Gillian took a deep breath, preparing her dive. Okay Gillian, remember what Mr. Campbell taught you in swimming class... But her cantation brought no useful memories. All she could remember was skipping P.E with her two friends, drinking coffee in Burger King...

 

Before she knew it, her bravery had propelled her to take the dive. In less than an instant, her skin made contact with water, blurring her vision. As Daniel promised, she floated right back up on the surface. Daniel swam towards her immediately and led her to a wall. "See? It wasn't that hard," Daniel said with a smile. "Now what do you say I teach you how to swim?"

 

"Really? You'd do that?" Gillian eyes sparked. She felt as though she was molding a childhood memory... Except, she wasn't a child. But to have her brother try to teach her how to swim! It made her emotional all of the sudden. "But I'm kinda dumb..."

 

"Nonsense! Everyone learns how to swim once they try!"

 

"I was in swimming class for a whole year..."

 

"Oh..." Daniel's hope deflated for a moment. "But then again, I am a great teacher."

 

"I make a better teacher than him." Edison suddenly appeared out of nowhere. Once he tugged at the wall, he turned to Gillian with cold, set eyes. "I'm pretty sure you'll feel safer with two teachers, right?"

 

"Why are you always bothering Gillian?" Daniel hissed, annoyed at Edison's interruption.

 

"Me? I think you're the one who's bothering her," Edison retorted. "But let's not waste time, yeah? I'm pretty sure Gillian is eager to get her first lesson!" Without warning, he pulled Gillian into the water with a force so strong she didn't have strength to fight back. Before she knew it, Edison was dragging her through the water. She found herself kicking her legs in a natural rhythm.

 

What is he trying to do? Gillian found her mind asking. She was sure he wasn't serious about teaching her how to swim. Hell, especially not after the callous threat she gave him!

 

Gillian became alarmed when they emerged onto the surface. That alarm transformed into panic when he encircled her into his arms with a tight hold. Just as she expected, he thrust her back into the water. The water, stank of chlorine, blared into her mouth like a mad flood. Some even went up her nose and tickled her throat. She was struggling under the water, with the obnoxious Edison trapping and anulling her escape. How dared he! He was trying to drown her!

 

The torture was surprisingly short as Gillian suddenly felt fresh air coming to her rescue. Horrible choking sounds erupted, and she realized they were coming from her as she clutched onto a wall and coughed all the water out. From the corner of her eyes, she spotted a smirking Edison.

 

"How'd you like that?" Edison asked smugly.

 

Gillian was still coughing. Her face had become red and swollen from the lack of oxygen and strenous attempts to breathe.

 

"Hey... It wasn't even that long..." Edison said, worry closing in on him when she wouldn't stop coughing. "Come on, don't tell you can't even stand that? What a weakling!"

 

She glared at him.

 

"Hey... Are you alright?" His concern was multiplying at her silence. He trotted towards her. "Look, I was just trying to scare you."

 

"Fucking idiot," she cursed. "You are the most childish man ever to disgrace this planet." She was so mad he could almost see smoke emitting from her ears.

 

Edison scratched his head. Guilt had halted his usual venomous self to spit back fiery insults. "Well... if you weren't trying to flirt with Daniel, it wouldn't have happened!" He defended himself.

 

"Oh? So if I were kissing him instead, you would have considered murdering me?"

 

"Nobody said such things!" He blurted back. "Like I said, I was just trying to scare you!"

 

"Well, then, it's fair game if I get you back!" In the heat of the moment, Gillian completely forgot she didn't know how to swim and lunged forward towards him. Her body lost control as it delved into the body of the water. Edison, acting on instincts, grabbed her and swam her back to the wall.

 

Gillian was breathing heavily once her face escaped the water. She was against the wall, with Edison pressing against her chest. One of his hand was gripping onto the wall while her legs wrapped themselves around his waist unknowingly. "Are you dumb or what?" Edison snarled at her. "You're brave as shit, but I do not intend on being a lifeguard here--" He stopped short, observing her delicate features. Strands of wet hair cluttered on her face, but her flushed cheeks restored radiance to the disheveled look. He didn't know why, but her beauty seemed to have multipled in scope at that moment, especially when he felt the tremendous heat from her body in the cooling water. Gillian's breathing became even more shallow as both were aware of the electrifying shocks signaling between them.

 

"Gillian!" Daniel's voice sounded from a semi-distant direction, snapping the two out of their reverie. Edison instantly threw himself to the side.

 

"Are you okay?" Daniel asked worriedly. "I saw you coughing badly from the other side! Damn it, Edison, what did you do to her?!" He sent hazardous stares towards Edison's direction.

 

"I'm fine..." Gillian replied before Edison had the chance. "I'm going to get washed up." Pulling herself out of the pool, Gillian grabbed a towel and wrapped herself with it in haste, feeling the stares from both of the males.

 

"Damn it, what was that?" Gillian asked herself as she walked away, remembering that lingering gaze from Edison earlier. Her stomach twisted with butterflies at the images in her head.

 

Gillian flicked on the shower nob once she was in the shower room, letting the hot water scorch her skin. In the midst of everything, her mind wandered to the scenario. She didn't know why the butterflies in her stomach hadn't abated. But their position earlier had felt so natural it bugged her. Damn it, Gillian, get those dirty thoughts out of your head, she scolded herself.

 

After she was done with the shower, she got dressed in atheltic shorts and a white t-shirt. To her surprise, Daniel was waiting outside the bathroom. If it were another person, Gillian would have found him creepy, even stalkerisque, but since this was Daniel, all malicious presumptions dispersed.

 

"Hey, you got dressed," Gillian noticed Daniel was no longer clad in swimwear.

 

"Yeah... You said you wanted to meet my mom and dad, so I thought we'd greet them for a bit."

 

"W-what?" Gillian's mind spun without control as mixed emotions settled in. She had been so eager and well-prepared to meet her father, but after discovering that letter, it had reduced to sheer resentment. She wasn't sure if she wanted to see her father at that instant. Unfortunately, she wasn't given much choice nor time. In just a moment, she found herself face to face with Jared on a sofa.

 

"Hey dad," Daniel greeted. "Where's mom?"

 

"She's in the kitchen. Daniel, who is this young lady? I've never seen her around before." Jared's expression was a curious one; even then, she could detect the amiable friendliness, which is a stark contrast to the image brewing inside her head.

 

"I'm Gillian, nice to meet you..." Gillian forced the formal words out of her mouth. Inside, she was burning to lash out at the old mad in front of her, to ask him endless questions and confirm her accusations. All of those feelings that were bottled up over the years threatened to regurgitate. But it was simply too much... Too much that spilling had become nearly impossible.

 

Jared nodded, turning back to Daniel. "What is the meaning of this pool party, son? I suggest you pay more attention to your studies."

 

"I know, I know. Chill, dad. We're just enjoying our youth," Daniel responded.

 

"Well... You better enjoy it because it'll be gone before you know it," Jared said glumly. "I'm going to the study room. And Gillian is it? I hope you're having fun."

 

He left Daniel and Gillian in a haze. The man was nice, but he still owned an aura of authority that Gillian respected. Somehow, her resentment had ceased its edge when she witnessed his wrinkled face of wisdom and compassion. Amazing how you could pick up a few things from a short encounter.

 

"Gillian? What do you think of him?" Daniel asked.

 

"He's... nice..." Gillian faltered. For some reason, she felt incredibly fatigued. Perhaps, too many emotions had toiled with her heart today. All she wanted at that moment was a peaceful nap, something she knew was wishful thinking givenhe situation. Her head unknowingly found its sanctuary on his shoulder.

 

"That's great!" Daniel enthused, a bit jittery a bit thrilled that they were so close together. "I'm really glad..." Warmness caroused his cheeks as he savaged their intimacy. Somehow, Gillian gave him a peaceful feeling that he thoroughly enjoyed. He had never felt it with anyone before; such notion only strengthened his fondness for her. That was when he realized his crush for this Gillian Chung may be more than simply puppy love. "Gillian...?" He beckoned for her attention, a sudden courage enveloping his mindset. "Do you feel comfortable around me?"'

 

She didn't offer an answer, but Daniel felt compelled to continue to finish his thought. "It feels nice to be around you... But I think it's more that that, don't you agree?" So many things materialized themselves in Daniel's mind, and he realized what he wanted. "Gillian, would you like to be my girlfriend?"

 

Again, silence. It was beginning to alarm him. Daniel's eyes glanced over at Gillian, and her serene expression softened him. The fact that she was sleeping didn't bother him at all. A smile crept its way to his lips as he leaned back onto the sofa, closed his eyes, and let sleep devour him.
