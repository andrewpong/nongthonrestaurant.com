Chapter 11

"Merry Christmas... friend"

It was December 25th. Ariel could not remember when was the last time Christmas had felt so... crowded.

From every corner of her house filled with guests. There were some that she knew, some that she invited, and some she barely recognized. She didn't miss those glaring looks from numerous girls either. Didn't some of them go to her school? Ariel did not remember inviting them but she figured after the announcement that she and Joe were dating, they were all furious. Maybe they were planning to murder her tonight.

"Ariel!" Rainie and Gillian appeared from the packed sardines. "I can't believe it. Since when did you become so popular?" Rainie asked, amazed at the number of people. "It's like... my god!"

Gillian nodded in agreement. "You've been very hot lately, Ariel. A few days ago before school ended, you were the topic of every conversation."

"Yeah, hot. Every girl in school practically hates me now," Ariel said with a tinge of sarcasm. "After the Jolin showdown, I am seriously not in any mood for more fan attacks."

"Dating Joe is like dating a celebrity, isn't it?" Rainie giggled. "Gosh, I'm envious."

"Wanna switch places?" Ariel asked.

"Actually, no. I still want to live."

Grandfather Lin and Rilla appeared out of nowhere, staring at Ariel with amazed looks on their faces. "Ariel, dear. I never knew you had so many friends!" Grandfather Lin exclaimed.

"I am so proud of my daughter!" Rilla beamed.

Ariel weakly smiled. Friends? Hah. They all want to kill me...

"Say, Ariel... Where is your boyfriend?" Rilla asked, shooting her knowing looks. Ariel had told her the whole thing with Joe, and despite it being all a facade, the woman was still very curious and interested to meet this alleged Cold Prince of the school.

Ariel had invited him of course. At first, she was against the idea since he was not the type who liked these kinds of things, but knowing that it would arouse suspicions, especially from her friends, she reluctantly did so. But whether he was coming or not, she was uncertain.

That uncertainty instantly cleared away when she caught sight of Mrs. Cheng.

"Grandfather Lin!" Mrs. Cheng exclaimed, approaching the group when a bored and stiffened Joe at her trails. He didn't look like he wanted to be there.

"Why, Mrs. Cheng," the old man replied, gleaming with excitement. "I was anticipating your presence! And of course, Ariel's boyfriend." He stroke his beard, nodding at Joe who politely nodded back in acknowledgement, even though he seemed to be detesting the title.

"What?!" Mrs. Cheng immediately opened her eyes in surprise. "Joe and Ariel are going out?!" She turned to her son, asking for a confirmation but he only looked away with obvious discomfort. Ariel could see he wasn't about to admit to anything.

"Yes, yes, we are," Ariel said with a smile.

"My God! I knew it!" Mrs. Cheng exclaimed. "Ever since that temple date, I have been waiting for this day to come! What I didn't expect is for it to happen so soon!!" She was laughing now, glowing with exuberance. Ariel immediately stiffened.

"Let's leave the children alone, shall we?" Rilla suggested. The adults agreed and decided to leave. Rilla decided to chuck Joe a wink before she left, which completely baffled him.

Once they left, Rainie immediately stepped up to Joe and Ariel. Ariel gulped, ready for the girl's attack. "You guys went on a DATE?" She asked, eyeing them harshly. "Why, you never told me!!!! Do you even take me as a friend?"

Joe glanced at Ariel, grasping that she had not told them about their date. He expected her to have spilled it long ago, but now, he was quite surprised to find out she didn't.

"Naughty, naughty, naughty!" Rainie exclaimed. "So you two have already fallen for each other after a few days?!?!"

"Uhhh...." Ariel stuttered. "Maybe?"

"Oh my god!" Rainie shook her head. "This challenge was obviously so easy for you!!"

"It's not over yet," Gillian reminded.

"I have absolutely no hope," Rainie sighed pessimistically. "I can't believe the cold prince could fall for a girl like Ariel so easily!"

Joe looked like he wanted to strangle Rainie. "Umm..." Ariel said. "Hey, where's your new boyfriend, Rainie? I want to meet him!"

"He's busy--hey, don't try to change the subject!" Rainie hissed. "Shouldn't you guys... you know, exchange gifts?"

"Gifts?" Ariel asked, bewildered. She had not thought about getting Joe anything, and likewise, Joe didn't either. "Uh, later ok?"

"What? Don't tell me you guys didn't get anything!" Rainie looked surprised. "What kind of couple are you?"

"A couple who doesn't need to give each other gifts," Ariel nodded.

"Bullshit!" Rainie shot back. "Which couple doesn't do that? You know... Now that I think about it... You two don't really look like you're going out... For God's sake, you don't even hang with each other at school much..." The suspicion was multiplying and Ariel witnessed it very clearly in Rainie's scrutinizing eyes.

"What are you talking about?" Ariel snapped. "Stop trying to be a reporter and dissecting our relationship okay? We are very much in love, for your information!" To demonstrate her point, she grabbed onto Joe's arm. He looked completely annoyed and uncomfortable. "Right, Joe?"

Joe looked down at her. Her eyes were demanding him to play along. Only then did he reluctantly nod.

"Oh really?" Rainie asked. "Okay! Why don't you two hug in front of that Christmas tree so I can take pictures for you?" She offered with a sweet tone and wide smiles. "We should make some memories for you two, shouldn't we?"

Ariel wasn't sure, but was this some sort of test? "Pictures?? Uh... Joe doesn't like pictures!"

"Why?" Rainie was persistent.

"Because... because he looks like crap on pictures," Ariel popped up a lame reason. Joe tossed her an outraged look, growing more annoyed by the second. "Yup, that's right. You don't know, Rainie, but he looks terrible and I mean terrible on camera."

"I'm leaving," Joe decided that he wasn't going to stay and listen to the girls' boring chat anymore. Rainie and Gillain instantly chucked him odd looks. Sure he was snobby, but shouldn't he accompany his girlfriend? The notion only rattled on Rainie's suspicion even more.

"You two are not really going out, huh?" Rainie glared at Ariel. "You two are faking it. I just know it."

"W-what? What are you talking about?" Ariel pretended to look shocked. Rainie did not have the chance to retort before a swarm of girls made their way towards Ariel. Apparently, these girls were taking advantage of Joe's disappearance.

"Ariel Lin," one of the girl said with utter disgust rolling out of her mouth. "I can't believe Joe is going out with this ugly girl."

"For real! Look at her! No boobs at all!"

"Wei!" Ariel snapped. "Who are you to criticize me like that?"

"Seriously. Take a look at yourself, bitches. You aren't any better!" Rainie snapped.

"Bitch, what did you just say?"

"Yeah, slut. I dare you to say that again."

All of them looked like they were about to pounce on Rainie and Ariel. "What? Are you scared?" And the girls roared into hyena laughter.

Ariel and Rainie exchanged glances before simultaneously shrilling together, "You're all ugly! U-G-L-Y!!!!!"

"Why you fucking bitches--"

"Run!" Gillian hissed.

Like three dogs fleeing danger, the girls picked up their feet and made a dash down the hallway, knocking into several guests. The pack of hyenas were trailing at their heels, spitting out names and demanding them to stop. Some of them even attempted to throw their heels. One slammed into a middle aged man's head and he was yelling the crap out of the culprit.

"Damn it! I never heard of running away and hiding in my own house!!" Ariel spat, lifting her dress to ease her running. The mob was still cursing like mad behind them. "I sure as hell am not inclined to get beaten up like a pulp again!"

"That stupid bunch of hyenas! Why the hell did you invite them?!" Rainie blared.

"I didn't!!"

"This isn't working! We gotta split up and distract them!" Gillian said.

Exchanging looks, the girls nodded and went their separate ways. Rainie decided to run upstairs, while Gillian fled into dining room. Ariel scammed into backyard where emptiness and loneliness lingered since no one wanted to be out in the coldness. Ariel didn't either. She was shivering like crazy and was about to return to the house when she caught sight of Joe sitting against the tree.

Curious, Ariel approached him. When he saw her, his expression immediately turned glum.

"Aren't you cold?" Ariel asked.

"Why are you here? Shouldn't you entertain your guests?" Joe asked.

"And get myself killed?" She said aloud, before taking a seat beside him. The wind was harsh and her hair was flapping all over the place. "I'm going to be killed out here too..." she murmured.

Joe smirked. "If you can't bear it out here, then don't force yourself."

"Wei, who said I can't?!" Ariel hissed back. "Stop looking down on people, sheesh." But her actions completely betrayed her words as her arms found their way to wrapping around her.

"Stop being so stubborn," he replied, noticing her shivering. "You don't have a jacket on. Better go back in."

"Stop telling me what to do!" She snapped. She hadn't meant to sound so hot headed; it must be the coldness that was getting into her. "You can always lend me your jacket you know..."

Before she knew it, she felt his sweater tossed over her shoulders. Ariel was completely taken aback at his action. Sure it was exactly what she said, but she hadn't exactly meant it.

"Just keep it. I'm not that cold," he said, as if reading her thoughts.

"Oh..." Almost immediately, she felt guilty for snapping at him earlier. "Um... thank you." But was he serious? She was freezing to death! How could he not be cold? Ariel couldn't bear to let him suffer. "Actually, never mind. It's your jacket..." She shoved it towards him, but he pushed it back.

"You wanted me to lend my jacket. I did! Now you're giving it back to me. Are you an idiot or what?" Joe said vexingly.

"I..." Ariel was stammering. "But it's so cold out here-"

"Then go back in!"

"But I want to accompany--"

"Nobody is asking you to."

"But-"

"Okay, that's enough. Stop bothering me will you?" It was clear Joe's patience had totally vanished. "I'm getting sick of seeing you, with your friends continuously pestering me and all. I'm tired of having girls coming up to me and asking me why I'm dating a girl like you. Ever since I met you, life has turned around for the worse. The last thing I want is responsibility of your health adding to my list of problems."

Ariel bit her lips, his harsh tone grating her. Her attempt at concern had obviously fire backed. However, she decided not to blow it out of proportions. "Fine," she said, submitting. Joe was a bit taken aback because he expected her to throw a tantrum. "I'm leaving then."

"Thank God," Joe murmured, his tone wounding her.

She stood up, sucking in a breath. "I just wanted to have a chat, and maybe you know, just spend a little time together for the holiday. Besides you were all alone. I was stupid to care for a jerk like you who doesn't even give a damn about me. I thought we were friends. Apparently, I thought wrong."

God, she must have been crazy. Who was she kidding? Joe Cheng didn't need anyone to care for him. After all, he was the cold prince--the unfeeling, the untouchable and unreachable. She was silly to think she could thaw him.

Ariel went back to the house, feeling depressed as ever. God, stupid Ariel. Why are you so flustered about this guy?! Who cares if he didn't want you as a friend? It's his loss.

Ariel soothed her nerves. But god, it hurt. She couldn't understand why. He had rejected her numerous times; was this any different? Why, then, was the pain so much more... heavy? It was close to the point of being unbearable. Ariel cursed, mesmerizing herself that she couldn't possibly have any feelings for this guy. No. After the contract was signed, all feelings had been chopped off.

"Look, you're all alone and abandoned now. Where are your friends, huh?" Suddenly, two girls approached Ariel with sinister smirks. They were right in front of the door leading to the backyard, so there weren't anybody nearby.

"What do you want?" Ariel spat, annoyed.

"How about your submission?" One of the girls snarled. "Give up, Joe, bitch."

"Sure, have him all you want!" Ariel snapped. "I don't give a damn." She brushed pass them, but her rudeness had grated the girls far too much for them to dismiss her.

"Wei, come back here," the same girl hissed, grabbing Ariel back. "How dare you talk to me in that tone! What do you take me as? A begger?!"

"Aren't you?" Ariel sneered back. "Begging for your prince Joe? Well, tell you what. He's available! I'm giving it to you!"

"You fucking slut," The girl grabbed Ariel. "You don't talk to me like that!"

"And -you- don't touch my girlfriend like that." Suddenly, Joe appeared behind them. His frost eyes immediately stopped all electrical activity of the girls' hearts. As he made his way towards her, Ariel was turned dumbfounded.

"Joe--" the same girl attempted to explain to him but he completely ignored her as he slipped his hands into Ariel's and jerked her away from the scene, ignoring the girls' calling after them.

"W-what are you doing--whoa!" Ariel almost lost her balance when Joe pulled her into the living room, where the teens were all singing karaoke. The adults were in the dining room, chattering among themselves, but when they saw Joe and Ariel, the couple of everyone's interest and curiosity, their conversations immediately came to a halt as they wondered what was going on.

Joe grabbed a microphone from a boy's hands. With a calmly poised and dynamic tone, he spoke, "I don't know why people are still so flustered about my matters, but I'm telling everyone now. Ariel is my girlfriend, and nothing is going to change that. So unless you want to offend me, stop harassing my girlfriend."

His speech was short and curt, but it was enough to blow everyone's mind and break every girl's heart that wasn't Ariel's. She was completely shocked and could only stare at him in speechlessness. He, on the other hand, harbored no exact emotion.

"Oh my...‚Äù Gillian exclaimed among a sudden burst of murmurs among the crowd. Rainie's jaw had dropped to the floor.

Joe pulled Ariel in front of the Christmas tree, hugging her small frame from behind. He turned to Rainie, his vexed expression surfacing. "Wei, are you going to take the pictures or not?!" He snapped frostily.

Rainie's lips were quivering as she nodded and prepared her camera, still speechless as ever.

But nobody was more speechless than Ariel was. As he pulled her closer towards him, she could feel the thunderous beating of her heart and the hotness that was flushing her face. They were so intimate in proximity she could feel the warmth emanating from his body. What just happened? Was this a dream? Everything felt so surreal.

She didn't want to think about such matters at the moment. Right now, she just wanted to cherish this heartwarming closeness.

"Merry Christmas... friend," he slowly whispered into her ears, making her slightly jump in his embrace but he only smirked as he pulled her closer.

Gradually, Ariel found her normalcy to bear smiles at the camera.

It was probably the best gift she'd ever received.
