Chapter 7

The Frost Eating the Flames

Ariel was standing in the hallways by the library, impatiently waiting for Joe so that they could proceed their study session. He had announced after class that he needed fifteen minutes before he could come down with her to the library. The curious Ariel couldn't help but wonder what he could be doing because as she distinctly recalled, he asked the same thing last time.

Shrugging, Ariel decided to brush it off, knowing her place. She had no right to butt into his business. It wasn't like she was all that interested either. The only reason she was adamant about attending the study session was because she believed more time spending together with him would eventually lure him to an agreement of a relationship. A fake one, of course.

Chances might be slim, considering Joe's tough and icy exterior that seemed to isolate him off of all ties of connection. But when Ariel recalled the way he'd shared his feelings with her that morning in the woods, she knew that it couldn't be all that difficult to penetrate into his mind.

Speaking of their date, Ariel remembered Joe's taunting eyes as he warned her to seal her mouth shut. Although she hated the way he thought he could use that dominance on her, Ariel was not a person to go back on her words.

So far, Ariel was successful in her attempt at avoidance. No one was casting her a suspicious stare or anything of that sort, which was a relief on her part. She knew she was being too alert, but she couldn't help the anxiety of being caught and then intently interrogated about her weekend with the school's most sought-after cold prince.

Especially her friends. God, Rainie and Gillian were probably turning the school upside down just looking for her. She knew they'd fire questions about her progress with the deal. Right now, she didn't want to answer any of their concerns, lest carelessness would cost her to spill details about her weekend with Joe.

Ariel had promised him that she'd keep her mouth shut. Knowing her clumsiness, she'd figured she should avoid any risky circumstance for now.

However, fate was against her because just as soon as Ariel thought about Rainie and Gillian, they simultaneously appeared out of nowhere, approaching her.

"So there you are!" Rainie's sharp voice sauntered to her ears.

"What are you two doing here?" Ariel asked. "School's out. Knowing you, you'd either be at a game, or somewhere in the malls. And Gillian, at home or something."

Gillian tilted her glasses. "I will be leaving in a moment. We were just passing by when we saw you standing in front of the library like a statue. Where were you at lunch, anyway?"

"If I didn't know better, I'd think that you're evading us!" Rainie said.

Ariel scowled at Rainie's words. It seemed like her friends were quick to catch the suspicious vibes. "Oh, uh... I had to stay after class to finish work."

"Wah... Seriously?!!?" Rainie could hardly contain her shock as she clutched tightly onto Ariel. "Why in the world would you, of all people, comply with your teacher's demand?"

"Well, he made me!" Ariel quickly lied.

"Knowing you, though, I would never expect that you'd comply to a teacher's request so easily." The wise Gillian was quick to catch the oddity of the situation, for nothing could escape her sharp observational skills. "Something sounds fishy."

"Right. So spill now!" Rainie demanded.

Ariel rolled her eyes, crossing her arms in a stubborn display of defiance. "No. There is nothing to say, anyway."

"Liar!" Rainie shot, two fierce eyes staring hard. "Remind me again why we're best friends. By now, I've picked up a couple of signs. So tell us, what have you been up to?"

Ariel bit back her lips. This was what she dreaded right from the beginning. Who'd know that fate was so cruel to have led her friends to sniff her out? If she were to spill out the details pertaining to the date with Joe, she would have to prepare to receive his merciless execution--whatever it might be.

But then she smiled. While Joe did warn her about keeping their date a secret, he said nothing about their partnership for the exams study session. "Oh, I'm meeting Joe right now. He agreed to help me study out of his own willingness." Ariel grinned.

"WHAT?!" Rainie screamed while Gillian's eyes bulged out in incredulity.

"This can't be," Rainie's eyes were round with outrage at the shocking revelation. "That was SO quick. How could you guys hook up together so fast?"

"Honestly, we aren't officially dating. But we might as well be," Ariel winked. "Prepare to sing in front of the school any day now, Rainie."

Ariel stifled a giggle. What her friends didn't know was Joe was associating with her only because Mr. Wang had paired them up. However, Ariel decided to keep quiet about that, and let Rainie squirm in anxiety at her potential loss for the bet. Victory was almost hers, anyway. Telling Rainie now could only serve as mere warnings of her impending lose. The girl was going to have to prepare for a big blow.

"You..." Rainie almost choked in disbelief. "Gillian! You're a smart one. Enlighten me with your interpretations! She's lying, isn't she? Joe cannot be possibly interested in her!"

"Taking the cold prince's reputation into account, he can't possibly agree to hang out with Ariel if there aren't feelings involved..." Gillian tilted her glasses as she plunged in speculation. "It seems as though Ariel has him trapped in the palm of her hand."

Horror swept across Rainie's features. "Impossible!"

"Oh give it up. It's a definite win for me," Ariel winked.

"Not so fast though..." Rainie spoke up, her eyes sparkling with a sinister gleam. "I guess it's time for me to interfere."

"I see the gears in your head are working over-time," Gillian observed, concerned about the risk of Rainie's new idea.

Rainie rolled her eyes. "Please. This is MY bet and I have the right to do whatever means to win it!"

"That's not fair. You cannot just decide to do that when I'm at the most critical stage!" Ariel strongly protested.

Rainie smirked sinisterly. "Hey. We have never stated that tricks are not allowed, have we? You are putting in a tremendous amount of effort to win this bet. Why can't I do the same, huh?"

"You, loser!" Ariel shot exasperatedly.

"SKANK!" Rainie stuck her tongue out.

"I don't know if you're even my friend! If you're helping me to get over Mike, why make this so difficult?!" Ariel wanted to scream.

Rainie rolled her eyes. "It's more fun this way, don't you think? Besides, the harder it is for you to win, the better it feels when you reap the rewards. Don't you agree, Gillian?"

"Yes."

A victorious grin contorted Rainie's lips as she tugged Gillian along. "Okay, then Ariel. We'll see who'll be singing in front of the school..." With a mischievous wink, they disappeared down the hallways.

Urg! Stupid Rainie. Ariel wanted to curse at her friend so badly. How could she do this? How? Disgruntled, Ariel whirled on her heels, suddenly remembering that she was supposed to meet Joe now. What was taking that jerk so long?

"You are aware that you're standing there like an idiot." Joe's voice nearly put a stop to her beating heart.

Ariel whipped on her heels and saw the usual indifference on his face. "Whoa. What are you doing there behind me? You know antics like those can give people a heart attack?"

"Only those who have a guilty conscience would feel that way."

"Exactly what are you trying to imply?" Ariel asked irritatingly.

Ariel peered at those chilling amber eyes that daunted so many and gasped audibly. Anxiety made her fingers curl. Could he have heard their conversation?

"Ummm..." Ariel was at the loss for words.

"Come. Let's go," Joe said simply, heading toward the front door.

Ariel gazed at him quizzically. "The library's here."

"Are you such an idiot that you can't even see it's closed?" Joe asked.

Embarrassment suffused Ariel's cheeks when she glanced back and realized he was right. She probably was too caught up in her moment to see the sign. "Oh... Where are we going to study then?" When she asked that question, she certainly wasn't expecting a surprise.

"My house."

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Ariel hadn't recovered from the surprise when Joe announced they were going to study at his house. It was hard to believe that such a cold prince like him was willing to take it that far. Just what exactly was his motive?

Her cynical brain wouldn't let go of all those speculations she came up with. Perhaps, he wanted to bring her to his house to rape her?! Such idea was always popular in movies and such...

Ariel smacked herself for her momentary stupid thinking. She was definitely putting her head in the gutter. Why would she want to do that?!

Joe must have noticed her absent-minded look because he began to talk. "We're neighbors, so I guess this isn't such a big deal as it sounds. By the way, I dislike the idea of coming to your room again. I feel more comfortable at my place."

Yeah, he just had to explain it so thoroughly, didn't he? Not to mention, he loved to seize any opportunity to throw her an insulting remark. Sure, she remembered the day when he came over to her house and intrusively entered her room, commenting about her lack of organization and cleanliness while he was at it. Stupid jerk.

"That's good because I don't want you in my room either," Ariel rebutted defensively, rolling her eyes at him.

Shortly, the door to Joe's house flew open, revealing a cheerful face of a middle-aged woman. Her eyes sparked pleasantly at the sight of Ariel. "Oh my! Ariel, youâ€™re too kind to pay us a visit!"

"Pleased to see you again, Mrs. Cheng," Ariel smiled politely.

"Mrs. Cheng sounds too awkward and old!" Joe's mother said, beaming with cheekiness. "You can just call me Auntie."

Joe butted in. "Ma... That's too intimate, don't you think?"

"Nonsense!" Joe's mother snapped. "We're neighbors, anyway. It's all right to be intimate with each other. Right, Ariel?"

Ariel nodded hesitantly. "Yes... Auntie."

"Come in, come in!" The older woman enthusiastically ushered them inside. She shut the door and turned to face them, clasping her hands together. It was obvious she was delighted to have a guest over. "So, what are you guys doing?"

"We're going to study," Joe honestly replied. "So it'll be convenient if you don't interrupt us."

"Of course not!" Mrs. Cheng giggled to herself, causing confusion to usurp both Ariel and Joe's expressions. "I know better than that..."

Even though Mrs. Cheng's assumption was obviously untrue, Ariel couldn't help the blush that suffused her cheeks at the implication. There was an urge to clarify but then that would only worsen the situation, considering how much it'd show that she was thinking inappropriately.

"What are you standing there for? Come up!" Joe berated from upstairs.

Ariel followed, grumbling inside.

His room was decorated simply with plain, dark posters pasted against blank walls. Its large interiority guaranteed plenty of empty space. There was only a bed, a set of drawers, and a desk. It also amazed Ariel how neatly and organized he was. There was not even a scratch of paper on the floor. The wooden surface was wiped clean without a stain of dusk. Was it her imagination, or did Ariel really see a shimmering spark somewhere?

"Where should we start?" Ariel asked, digging through her bag for her English binder.

"Continue from where we left off," Joe replied as he took a seat at the desk.

"Wei..." Ariel suddenly realized she was still standing. "Where am I supposed to sit? There's only one desk... and no extra chair."

"Try the bed," Joe said.

Ariel gulped. "Bed?"

He probably sensed her concern. "Why? Are you afraid you might be enticed by the smell of my sheets?"

"No!" Ariel quickly snapped crossly, not believing he was in such a mood to say something so dirty. "Not everyone thinks you're the perfect guy, you know."

"Whatever. If you don't want to, then try the floor."

Ariel gritted her teeth. Couldn't he accommodate her for once? Couldn't he act nice? Even though he had a reputation as a cold prince, it did not give him a reason to neglect his female guest and let her sit on the floor!

"Wei!! Why can't you sit on the bed and leave the desk to me?" Ariel complained.

"This is my house, and I'll do as I wish."

"I am your guest!"

Joe must have known that Ariel was stubborn because he suddenly stormed out of the room and returned moments later with a chair. He slammed it down next to his. "There, we'll study side by side then."

"I didn't ask for that--"

"Are you sitting or not?" He snapped.

"Fine."

For the next hour or so, the two were completely in studying mode, as none uttered a word. Ariel wanted to ask him a couple of questions concerning the work, but seeing how he was so concentrated made Ariel hesitant, lest he might snapped at her. It was only a few more days before the day of the exam finally approached. No wonder he was working so hard. Ariel found herself fascinated by his strong motivation to succeed in life. His character was totally the opposite from hers. While he was suffering his ass off under all this pressure of getting good grades, she was slacking off in unrealistic dreams and fantasies. While he buried himself inside the library, she sneaked off to arcades and games. There was definitely a huge difference in their lives.

Ariel glanced back at the English book, her mind twirling at the thousand of words taunting at her. How could people do these things?! She asked inside her mind. She couldn't even keep her attention on the book for more than ten minutes before she had to quickly succumb to headaches.

Time was passing by, yet Ariel was not even absorbing anything. This was fruitless. No matter how much she tried, she just couldn't focus!

Ariel decided to spark a conversation. "Say... Joe... What were you doing after school? You always asked for fifteen minutes before we can start our session..."

Joe stopped what he was doing, perhaps surprised at her sudden question.

Realizing the awkwardness, Ariel fumbled. "Oh... um... I was just curious, that's all. If it's too personal, never mind."

"I went to the music room."

Shocked, Ariel gazed at him. "Music room?" Her mind wandered back to the day when she saw him playing the piano... How could she forget? The beautiful melody enthralled her beyond words. For sure, she would never forget that melodious tune... "You play the piano every day?"

He nodded.

"Jeez," Ariel said, quite grudgingly impressed. "You're smart... and you know how to play instruments! Man, what don't you know how to do???"

"It's just for fifteen minutes. I have to play it to feel calm." There was a certain melancholy in his eyes, Ariel noticed.

She suddenly remembered the conversation they shared in the woods. He mentioned something about a girl. Could this have been related to her?

"You miss her, don't you?" Ariel asked.

He was quite surprised that she brought it up.

"I know you guys separated. But can't you two still keep in contact? Or something happened, didn't it?"

Joe seemed quiet for a moment, as though contemplating if he should divulge his inner thoughts. Even though they weren't exactly on best-buddy terms, Ariel believed that after the conversation they had, it shouldn't be that difficult to speak about this now. Truthfully, she was quite interested about this girl, and how she got Joe so smitten about her.

However, he disappointed her. "Nothing. An idiot like you wouldn't understand anyway."

Ariel flinched. What was that? Why did he have to resort to calling her names? Suddenly, she felt her temper rising with an unstoppable force. "Excuse me?"

"I said, you wouldn't understand."

Her fists clenched and her jaw almost dropped. "How would you know if I wouldn't understand? You don't have to be so judgmental toward me! You don't even know me!"

Joe seemed quite taken back by her strong reaction. However, his voice suggested no guilt. "I don't need to explain my life to strangers. We're just here to study. Don't get too personal with me."

"Just what the hell is wrong with you?" Ariel asked. "I knew you'd show your true colors as a plain jerk that always puts on a front."

That got him on the defensive. "What are you trying to say?"

Ariel scoffed. "Nothing. You wouldn't want to listen to an idiot talking, anyway."

"I'm serious. You're hinting at something I don't quite exactly understand. Care to explain?"

Ariel grumbled. "I'm saying that you're a damn fake that puts on a front! There!"

Joe's icy eyes were penetrating through her skin.

"You're a first class jerk who belittles everyone else around you," Ariel elaborated. "You always show to the world how cool and smart you are and everyone always admire you for that. Yet inside, you hide your feelings and thoughts. You don't think anyone is worthy to get to know you. Why else would you act so arrogantly when I was just asking out of curiosity and perhaps, even concern?! You're pessimistic and cynical, and you doubt everyone's motive! People like you are better off buried in tombs!"

His eyes were piercing through her with a heavy burning malice. "You think that by that narrowed-minded view, you can conclude that I am some kind of fake, and you, the genius that knows it *all* ?" He scoffed irritably. "Don't get your nose out of joint."

"I'm not!" Ariel shouted. "You're just denying what I said is true!"

"Oh yeah? If I'm such a hateful person that you made me to be, then why are you so persistent to date me?" Joe yelled. "Of course! It turns out that you're just trying to satisfy your desperate need to show off *and* a stupid bet! I think you're the one better off buried in tombs!"

Comprehension cut off her breathing rapidly. So he did hear her conversation with Rainie after all. "You! You eavesdropped on me!!"

"Yeah, I was eavesdropping on a group of girls who are shameless enough to speak loudly in public places," he derided sarcastically.

"You..." Ariel sputtered. "You are so rude, do you know that?"

"Rude? At least I'm not that shameless to go begging for a date just because I'm desperate for my ex's attention."

Silence.

Joe looked toward Ariel, sensing that he might have gone a bit too far. Guilt suddenly trampled him hard. "Listen, our emotions aren't stable right now--"

"Way to jump over that fence, Joe!" Ariel retorted. "I don't want to speak to you anymore or hear empty apologies. You have a tendency to spit out words that always hurt me! I don't want to hear it!"

His expression was dead and cold. "Fine by me." He turned away.

"You see!" Ariel fumed. "You ignore others' needs because you're too absorbed with your own world. You may not want to admit it, but I can see through you more than you think I can--"

"Shut up," he grumbled.

"No!" Ariel yelled. "I'll have to get this through your thick head. The world doesn't revolve around you. You need to open your eyes and pay attention to others--"

"Enough! I don't need your rubbish! You may *think* I'm those stereotypical dark guys that perky girls, like you, think you can change, but you never, *ever* will!!"

"Perky girls like me?" Ariel repeated.

"You think you can fix me?" he asked, rolling his eyes, "Sorry to break it to you, but I'm not an object you can use to validate your accomplishments with, Ariel."

She fumed. "I'm not trying to fix you--"

"Stop being so stubborn! I don't need your pity and I don't need your friendship! Just get out! Get out of my sight! Get out of my house! And most importantly, get out of my life!" Joe shouted at the top of his lungs.

Shock ate her up entirely as Ariel raged in righteous indignation. She had never seen him react so fiercely before. It made her question herself whether or not she had stepped too comfortably in his territory. But still, even if she was stepping over the line, that did not give him the right to insult her like he did. No matter how low she might appear to him, Ariel couldn't accept his crude words and stabbing gestures toward her.

Stupid jerk, she kept cursing in her mind. How could she have agreed to chase after him for a date? He was heartless! A complete monster! It would be suicide to have him as a boyfriend.

Red-faced, Ariel decided to retreat with pride and dignity. She quickly dumped her stuff in her bag and marched out of the room, slamming the door on her way out.
